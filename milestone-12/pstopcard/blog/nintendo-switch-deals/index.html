How to Get the Best Deals

Beyond the current sales, here are some tips for getting the best prices on Nintendo eShop games year-round:

  1. Check the "Great Deals" section weekly - Nintendo updates their featured deals every Thursday.
  2. Wishlist games you're interested in - You'll get notifications when they go on sale.
  3. Consider digital gift cards - Sometimes you can find eShop credit at a discount from third-party sellers.
  4. Look for publisher sales - Companies like Capcom, Square Enix, and Ubisoft often have their own sales.
  5. Check before major holidays - Black Friday, Christmas, and summer often bring the biggest discounts.

Regional Price Differences

It's worth noting that eShop prices can vary significantly by region. Some regions consistently have lower prices than others. While Nintendo has cracked down on region switching, it's still possible to create accounts in different regions to take advantage of better pricing in some cases.

"The South African eShop often has the best prices for first-party Nintendo titles, sometimes 10-15% cheaper than North American prices even after currency conversion."

However, be aware that DLC must be purchased from the same region as the base game, so this strategy works best for complete editions or standalone games.

Physical vs. Digital Deals

While this guide focuses on eShop deals, it's worth comparing digital and physical prices:

  • Digital advantages: No shipping wait, no risk of losing cartridges, often better sales on indie titles
  • Physical advantages: Can be resold, sometimes deeper discounts at retailers, collectible cases

For first-party Nintendo titles, physical copies sometimes see deeper discounts at retailers like Amazon or Best Buy than the eShop offers. However, digital sales are becoming more frequent and substantial than in previous years.
