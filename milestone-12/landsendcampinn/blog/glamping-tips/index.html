Essential Packing List for Glamping

May 28, 2025 • 4 min read

What to bring (and what to leave at home) for the perfect glamping experience in our luxury pods at Land's End. Your complete guide to packing for comfort and style.

Glamping at Land's End combines the best of camping and hotel comforts, but knowing what to pack can make all the difference to your experience. Our luxury pods come equipped with many amenities, but there are still some essentials you'll want to bring along. Here's our comprehensive packing guide to ensure your glamping holiday is as comfortable and enjoyable as possible.

What's Provided in Our Glamping Pods

Before we get to what you should pack, let's cover what you don't need to bring:

  • Bedding: All beds come with quality linens, duvets, and pillows
  • Kitchen essentials: Pods have a microwave, kettle, toaster, two-ring hob, fridge with freezer compartment, crockery, glasses, cutlery and basic cooking utensils
  • Bathroom: Each pod has its own shower, toilet, and hand towel
  • Heating: Wood burning stove (with starter pack) and electric heating
  • Extras: Tea towel, small welcome pack with tea/coffee/sugar

Essential Packing List

Clothing

Cornwall's weather can be changeable even in summer, so layers are key:

  • Base layers: Comfortable clothes for daytime
  • Warm layers: Fleece or jumper for cool evenings
  • Waterproof jacket: Essential year-round
  • Sturdy footwear: For coastal walks (wellies in winter)
  • Flip flops/sandals: For the shower and pod
  • Swimwear: For beach trips or our recommended swim spots
  • Hat & gloves: For winter stays or early/late season
  • Bath towels: We provide hand towels but not bath towels

Toiletries

While our pods have bathrooms, you'll need to bring:

  • Toothbrush & toothpaste
  • Shampoo, conditioner & shower gel
  • Skincare products
  • Hair dryer (if needed - we provide one in the shower block)
  • Any medications
  • Sunscreen (even on cloudy days)
  • Insect repellent (especially for summer evenings)

Food & Drink

Our pods have basic cooking facilities, but you might want to bring:

  • Your favorite tea/coffee (we provide a small starter pack)
  • Basic cooking ingredients
  • Snacks for walks and day trips
  • Wine/beer opener if needed
  • Cool bag/box for picnics
  • Reusable water bottles
"We loved cooking simple meals in our pod after long coastal walks. The hob and microwave were perfect for our needs." - Sarah, recent guest

Entertainment

While we hope you'll spend most of your time exploring, you might want:

  • Books/magazines
  • Travel games/cards
  • Tablet/laptop (though we encourage digital detox!)
  • Binoculars for wildlife spotting
  • Camera for capturing stunning sunsets

Tech Essentials

  • Phone charger
  • Camera & charger
  • Power bank for day trips
  • Headphones
  • Extension lead (pods have limited sockets)

Seasonal Additions

Summer (June-August)

  • Extra sunscreen
  • Sun hat
  • Beach towel
  • Cooling spray
  • Lightweight clothing

Spring/Autumn (March-May, September-October)

  • Waterproof trousers
  • Layered clothing
  • Thermal base layers (for early/late season)
  • Sturdy walking boots

Winter (November-February)

  • Thermal underwear
  • Warm hat, scarf, gloves
  • Thick socks
  • Waterproof boots
  • Hot water bottle (though pods are well insulated)

What Not to Bring

To maximize space and minimize stress, leave these at home:

  • Excessive kitchen equipment: Our pods have the basics
  • Towels: We provide hand towels (just bring bath towels)
  • Bedding: All provided
  • Hairdryer: Available in shower block
  • Firewood: Only wood purchased on site can be used
  • Large suitcases: Storage space is limited in pods

Packing Tips for Glamping

  1. Use packing cubes: Helps organize small spaces
  2. Pack light: Pods have limited storage
  3. Soft bags: Easier to store than hard suitcases
  4. Multi-purpose items: Like a sarong that can be a beach towel/blanket/scarf
  5. Check weather: Cornwall's climate can change quickly
  6. Leave space: For souvenirs and local purchases

Special Considerations

For Families

  • Baby wipes (useful for quick clean-ups)
  • Favorite snacks for kids
  • Compact travel toys
  • Night light if needed

For Couples

  • Romantic extras like candles (battery-operated only)
  • Wine glasses (we provide basic ones)
  • Picnic blanket

For Solo Travelers

  • Journal or sketchbook
  • Good book
  • Camera for capturing memories

Last-Minute Checklist

Before you leave home, double-check you have:

  • Booking confirmation
  • Directions to site
  • Key collection details
  • Emergency contact numbers
  • Any special dietary foods
  • Medications
  • Chargers for all devices

Remember, while our pods are well-equipped, they're designed to help you connect with nature and simplify your life. Packing light often leads to a more relaxing experience. If you do forget anything essential, Penzance is just a 15-minute drive away with shops for most necessities.

We hope this guide helps you prepare for your glamping adventure at Land's End. With the right packing, you'll be able to fully enjoy the stunning scenery, comfortable accommodation, and unique experiences our location offers.
