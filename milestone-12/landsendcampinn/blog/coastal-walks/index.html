Best Coastal Walks Near Land's End

June 12, 2025 • 5 min read

Discover the most breathtaking coastal paths just minutes from our campsite, with routes for all abilities. From easy family strolls to challenging hikes with spectacular views, Cornwall's coastline offers something for everyone.

Cornwall's coastline is renowned for its dramatic cliffs, secluded coves, and breathtaking views. At Land's End Camping & Glamping, we're perfectly positioned for you to explore some of the most spectacular coastal walks in the UK. Whether you're looking for a gentle family stroll or a challenging hike, there's a route to suit everyone.

1. Land's End to Sennen Cove

This is our most popular walk and for good reason. Starting just 10 minutes from our campsite, this 1.5-mile (each way) route offers some of the most iconic views in Cornwall.

Walk Details:

  • Distance: 3 miles round trip
  • Difficulty: Moderate (some steep sections)
  • Time: 1.5-2 hours
  • Highlights: Dramatic cliffs, Land's End landmark, Sennen Cove beach

The path begins at the Land's End visitor center and follows the South West Coast Path west towards Sennen Cove. Along the way, you'll pass the famous Land's End signpost (perfect for photos) and enjoy panoramic views across the Atlantic. The descent into Sennen Cove is steep but rewarded with one of Cornwall's most beautiful beaches - perfect for a post-walk swim or ice cream!

"The views from this walk are simply breathtaking. We did it at sunset and it was one of the highlights of our Cornwall trip." - Mark, recent guest

2. Sennen Cove to Nanjizal (Song of the Sea)

For those looking for a more secluded experience, this 2.5-mile walk (each way) takes you to one of Cornwall's hidden gems - Nanjizal Beach, also known as Song of the Sea.

Walk Details:

  • Distance: 5 miles round trip
  • Difficulty: Moderate to challenging
  • Time: 2.5-3 hours
  • Highlights: Secluded beach, natural rock formations, wildlife spotting

Starting at Sennen Cove car park, the path heads south along rugged cliffs with spectacular ocean views. Nanjizal is only accessible on foot, which keeps the crowds away. The beach features stunning rock formations including the famous "Song of the Sea" arch. Look out for seals and seabirds along the way!

3. Porthcurno to Minack Theatre

This gentle 1-mile walk combines coastal scenery with one of Cornwall's most unique attractions - the cliffside Minack Theatre.

Walk Details:

  • Distance: 2 miles round trip
  • Difficulty: Easy (paved path with some steps)
  • Time: 1 hour (plus time to explore the theatre)
  • Highlights: Minack Theatre, Porthcurno Beach, subtropical gardens

Park at Porthcurno beach (15 minute drive from campsite) and follow the well-maintained path up to the theatre. The views from the theatre's terraces are spectacular, and if you time your visit right, you might catch a performance! Combine this walk with a visit to the beach below - often voted one of the best in Cornwall.

4. Cape Cornwall Circular Walk

For a more challenging full-day hike, this 6-mile circular route takes in some of West Cornwall's most dramatic scenery.

Walk Details:

  • Distance: 6 miles circular
  • Difficulty: Challenging (steep climbs and rough terrain)
  • Time: 4-5 hours
  • Highlights: Cape Cornwall, mining heritage, wildflowers, birdwatching

Starting at St Just (10 minute drive), this walk takes you around the only cape in England (where two seas meet). The route passes old mining ruins and offers spectacular views back towards Land's End. The section between Cape Cornwall and Priest Cove is particularly beautiful, with wildflowers in spring and summer.

Walking Tips for Land's End

To make the most of your coastal walks, keep these tips in mind:

  1. Footwear: Proper walking boots with good grip are essential - paths can be uneven and slippery.
  2. Weather: Coastal conditions change quickly. Bring layers and waterproofs even on sunny days.
  3. Tides: Check tide times if visiting beaches - some become inaccessible at high tide.
  4. Supplies: Carry water and snacks - there are few facilities along most routes.
  5. Sun protection: The Cornish sun can be strong even on cloudy days.
  6. Dogs: Keep dogs on leads near cliff edges and livestock.

Best Times to Walk

While the coastal paths are beautiful year-round, each season offers different highlights:

  • Spring: Wildflowers, fewer crowds, cool walking temperatures
  • Summer: Long daylight hours, chance for post-walk swims (but busiest time)
  • Autumn: Dramatic seas, migrating birds, beautiful light
  • Winter: Storm watching (from safe distances!), peaceful paths

Early morning and late afternoon walks often provide the best light for photography and fewer people on the paths. Sunset walks are particularly spectacular - we can recommend the best viewpoints from our campsite.

Walking with Children

Many of our guests enjoy family walks along the coast. For younger children, we recommend:

  • Land's End to First & Last Point: Just 0.5 miles each way with amazing views
  • Sennen Cove beach walk: Explore rock pools at low tide
  • Porthgwarra to Porthcurno: 1.5 miles with the reward of ice cream at Porthcurno

Make walks more engaging for kids with wildlife spotting (we provide checklists), treasure hunts, or planning a picnic stop.

Guided Walks

For those who'd like expert local knowledge, several companies offer guided walks in the area:

  • Coastal Heritage Walks: Learn about the area's mining history and wildlife
  • Photography Walks: Capture the best shots with a professional photographer
  • Foraging Walks: Discover edible plants and coastal herbs (seasonal)

We can help arrange these when you book your stay with us.

After Your Walk

Back at Land's End Camping & Glamping, we offer:

  • Hot showers and drying room for wet gear
  • Campfire area to relax and share stories of your adventures
  • Local recommendations for pubs and cafes to refuel
  • Maps and guidebooks to plan your next walk

Whether you're an experienced hiker or just looking for a gentle stroll with incredible views, the Land's End area offers some of the finest coastal walking in the UK. We'd love to help you plan your perfect walking holiday - just ask when you book!
