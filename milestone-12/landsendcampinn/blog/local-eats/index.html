Food Local Guide

Best Local Eats Near the Campsite

May 15, 2025 • 7 min read

From fresh seafood to Cornish pasties, discover our favorite places to eat within 10 minutes of Land's End Camping & Glamping.

One of the great pleasures of visiting Cornwall is enjoying the fantastic local food. At Land's End Camping & Glamping, we're fortunate to be surrounded by excellent eateries serving everything from freshly caught seafood to authentic Cornish pasties. Here's our curated guide to the best dining options within 10 minutes of our campsite.

1. The Beach Restaurant - Sennen Cove

Just a 3-minute drive from our campsite, this beachfront gem offers the freshest seafood with stunning ocean views.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Daily caught fish and shellfish
  • Must-try: Their famous seafood platter (order for two)
  • Atmosphere: Casual beachfront dining
  • Good to know: Book ahead for sunset tables
"We ate here three times during our week's stay - the crab linguine was to die for!" - Emma, recent guest

2. The First & Last Inn - Sennen

This historic 17th-century pub is the first and last inn in England and just 5 minutes from our site.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Traditional pub fare with Cornish twists
  • Must-try: Their Sunday roast with all local ingredients
  • Atmosphere: Cozy, historic pub with log fires
  • Good to know: Dog-friendly with water bowls and treats

3. The Cornish Bakery - Multiple Locations

For authentic Cornish pasties, you can't beat this local chain with outlets in Sennen and Penzance.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Traditional and creative pasty varieties
  • Must-try: Classic steak pasty (certified authentic Cornish pasty)
  • Atmosphere: Quick takeaway or casual seating
  • Good to know: Great for packed lunches on coastal walks

4. The Beach Cafe - Porthcurno

A 10-minute drive brings you to this casual beach cafe with surprisingly gourmet offerings.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Beachside dining with local ingredients
  • Must-try: Their crab sandwiches with homemade bread
  • Atmosphere: Barefoot beach casual
  • Good to know: Perfect post-Minack Theatre visit

5. The Old Success Inn - Sennen Cove

Another excellent Sennen Cove option with a more refined dining experience.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Modern British cuisine with seafood focus
  • Must-try: Lobster thermidor (in season)
  • Atmosphere: Elegant but not stuffy
  • Good to know: Beautiful sea views from the dining room

6. The Apple Tree Cafe - St Just

A 10-minute drive inland to this charming market town reveals this vegetarian-friendly gem.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Creative vegetarian and vegan options
  • Must-try: Their famous cream tea with homemade scones
  • Atmosphere: Quirky, artsy cafe
  • Good to know: Many gluten-free options available

7. The Minack Cafe - Porthcurno

While visiting the famous Minack Theatre, don't miss their cliffside cafe.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Light lunches with spectacular views
  • Must-try: Their Cornish cream tea with theatre views
  • Atmosphere: Alfresco with breathtaking coastal scenery
  • Good to know: No need for theatre tickets to visit cafe

8. The Tolcarne Inn - Newlyn

Venture 15 minutes to Newlyn (Cornwall's largest fishing port) for this seafood institution.

Highlights:

  • Specialty: Ultra-fresh seafood straight from the boats
  • Must-try: Their daily changing fish specials
  • Atmosphere: Traditional Cornish pub with serious food
  • Good to know: Book well ahead - very popular

Best Budget Options

For those watching their spending:

  • The Sennen Cove Chippy: Excellent fish and chips to eat on the beach
  • St Just Market: Wednesday farmers market with affordable local produce
  • Penzance Lidl: For stocking up on camping essentials (15 min drive)

Best Splurge Restaurants

For special occasions:

  • The Shore Restaurant, Penzance: Michelin-starred seafood (20 min drive)
  • Ben's Cornish Kitchen, Marazion: Celebrated Cornish fine dining (25 min drive)

Local Food Experiences

Beyond restaurants, consider these culinary experiences:

  • Newlyn Fish Market Tour: See the morning auction (6am, 15 min drive)
  • Cornish Pasty Making Class: At the Penzance Cookery School
  • Local Vineyard Tour: Cornish wine is surprisingly good!

Our Top Tips for Dining in West Cornwall

  1. Book ahead: Even casual places get busy in season
  2. Try the local specialties: Look for Cornish sardines, crab, and hake
  3. Ask about catch of the day: The freshest fish isn't always on the menu
  4. Check opening times: Some places close early or have limited winter hours
  5. Consider lunch specials: Many places offer great value midday menus
  6. Bring cash: Some smaller places don't take cards

Cooking at Your Pod or Campsite

If you prefer to cook yourself, we recommend these local suppliers:

  • Newlyn Fish Market: Buy direct from the fishermen (early mornings)
  • St Just Butchers: Excellent local meats
  • Treasure Farm Shop: Organic local produce (5 min drive)

Remember, our glamping pods have basic cooking facilities (hob, microwave, fridge) and we have a communal BBQ area for camper use.

Final Thoughts

From casual beach cafes to gourmet dining rooms, the Land's End area offers an impressive range of eating options to suit all tastes and budgets. The common thread is a commitment to fresh, local ingredients - especially the incredible seafood caught daily off our shores. We're always happy to make personal recommendations based on your preferences - just ask when you check in!
