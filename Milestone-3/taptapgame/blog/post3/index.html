The Making of TapTap: Behind the Scenes

By Yusuf Öget April 10, 2025 8 min read Development

Early sketches and prototypes that shaped TapTap's development

Creating TapTap was a journey filled with creative challenges, technical hurdles, and moments of inspiration. In this behind-the-scenes look, I'll share the story of how a simple idea evolved into the addictive game you know today. From initial concept to final polish, discover the design decisions and development process that brought TapTap to life.

The Initial Inspiration

TapTap was born from a desire to create something simple yet challenging. I wanted to make a game that:

  • Could be understood in seconds but mastered over time
  • Tested pure reflex and timing skills
  • Had no complex controls or tutorials
  • Felt satisfying with every successful tap

"The best games often come from simple ideas executed exceptionally well. With TapTap, I wanted to strip away everything unnecessary and focus purely on that moment of perfect timing."

- Yusuf Öget, Developer

Designing the Core Mechanics

The heart of TapTap is its deceptively simple gameplay loop. Achieving the right balance took careful tuning:

1

Ball Rotation Speed

After testing various speeds, we settled on 2.5 rotations per second - fast enough to be challenging but slow enough to track visually.

2

Line Movement Patterns

The line changes position randomly but with weighted probabilities to create fair but unpredictable challenges.

3

Scoring System

Early versions had complex scoring, but we simplified to focus on consecutive perfect taps for maximum satisfaction.

Visual & Audio Design

The minimalist aesthetic was a deliberate choice to keep focus on gameplay:

Sound Design

The audio feedback was carefully crafted to provide satisfying confirmation of successful taps without becoming annoying during extended play sessions.

Success Tone

A bright, positive sound that rewards perfect timing

Fail Tone

A subtle but clear indication of mistimed taps

Background Music

Minimal ambient tracks that don't distract from gameplay

Challenges & Breakthroughs

The development process wasn't without its obstacles:

The "Too Hard" Problem

Early testers found the game frustratingly difficult. We solved this by:

  • Adding a brief visual highlight when the ball aligns with the line
  • Gradually increasing speed over the first few taps
  • Including the Zen mode for stress-free practice

Performance Optimization

Ensuring smooth performance across all devices required:

  • Rewriting the rendering code to use less battery
  • Implementing dynamic quality adjustments for older devices
  • Reducing memory usage by optimizing assets

Lessons Learned

Developing TapTap taught me valuable lessons about game design:

  • Simplicity is harder than complexity - every element must justify its existence
  • Playtesting is crucial - what makes sense to the developer might not to players
  • Polish matters - small details create the overall experience

Seeing people enjoy TapTap has made all the challenges worthwhile. This is just the beginning - I can't wait to share what's coming next!
