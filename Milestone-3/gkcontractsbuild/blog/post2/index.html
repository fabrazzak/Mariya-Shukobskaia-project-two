Essential Planning Tips for Your House Extension

Liam Bennett

May 15, 2025 • 5 min read

2,156 views

Planning a house extension in Banbridge or the surrounding areas? As experienced builders in Northern Ireland, we've compiled our top tips to help your project run smoothly from initial concept to completion.

1. Define Your Objectives Clearly

Before contacting architects or builders, take time to clearly define what you want to achieve with your extension:

  • What specific problems are you trying to solve? (e.g., more living space, larger kitchen)
  • How will the space be used now and in the future?
  • What's your must-have vs. nice-to-have features?
  • How will the extension affect your home's overall flow?

Having clear objectives will guide all subsequent decisions and help professionals provide accurate advice.

Pro Tip

"Create a scrapbook of design ideas you like. This visual reference helps communicate to architects and builders more effectively than verbal descriptions alone." - Michael O'Neill, Project Manager

2. Understand Planning Permission Requirements

In Northern Ireland, many extensions fall under permitted development, but restrictions apply:

  • Single-story extensions can't extend beyond 4m (detached) or 3m (other houses)
  • Maximum height of 4m
  • No more than 50% of garden can be covered
  • Different rules apply for listed buildings and conservation areas

We always recommend checking with your local council's planning department before finalizing designs.

3. Budget Realistically

Extensions in Northern Ireland typically cost between £1,200-£2,000 per m², depending on specifications. When budgeting:

  • Add 10-15% contingency for unexpected issues
  • Factor in professional fees (architect, surveyor, etc.)
  • Consider temporary accommodation if needed
  • Remember decoration and furnishing costs

4. Choose the Right Professionals

Your team can make or break your project. Essential professionals include:

Professional Role When Needed
Architect Design creation, planning applications From start
Structural Engineer Technical design, calculations After initial designs
Building Contractor Construction work After planning permission
Building Control Regulatory compliance Throughout project

5. Consider the Construction Phase

Prepare for the practical realities of construction:

  • Access for builders and deliveries
  • Storage for materials
  • Dust and noise protection for existing home
  • Safety measures if you have children or pets
  • Temporary kitchen setup if extending your current one

Most single-story extensions take 12-16 weeks, while two-story extensions typically require 16-20 weeks.

6. Future-Proof Your Design

Consider how your needs might change over time:

  • Flexible spaces that can adapt to different uses
  • Accessibility features (wider doors, level access)
  • Energy efficiency for rising fuel costs
  • Technology infrastructure (wiring, smart home features)

Final Thoughts

Proper planning is the foundation of a successful house extension. By addressing these key considerations early, you'll minimize stress, avoid costly mistakes, and ultimately achieve a space that enhances your home and lifestyle for years to come.
