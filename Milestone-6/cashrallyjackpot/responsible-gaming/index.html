Your Responsibility

As a player, responsible gaming means understanding that our games are for entertainment only and setting appropriate limits for yourself.

Remember: Never chase losses, don't play when upset or depressed, and balance gaming with other activities. Gaming should enhance your life, not dominate it.

Signs of Problem Gaming

Recognizing potential warning signs can help maintain healthy gaming habits

Time Spent

Spending more time or money on gaming than you can afford, or finding it hard to stop

Priorities

Neglecting work, studies, family or friends to spend time gaming

Emotions

Feeling restless or irritable when trying to cut down on gaming

Financial Impact

Spending money meant for essentials like bills or groceries on gaming

Secrecy

Lying to family or friends about time or money spent gaming

Mood

Gaming to escape problems or relieve feelings of helplessness or depression

Chasing

Trying to win back losses by continuing to play ("chasing" losses)

Borrowing

Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gaming

If you recognize several of these signs in yourself or someone you care about, it may indicate a gambling problem.

Gaming Self-Assessment

Answer these simple questions to evaluate your gaming habits

1. Have you ever missed work or other important commitments because of gaming?

2. Have friends or family expressed concern about your gaming?

3. Have you ever lied about how much time or money you spend gaming?

4. Do you feel restless or irritable when you try to cut down on gaming?

5. Have you ever used gaming to escape problems or negative feelings?

If you answered "Yes" to two or more questions, you may want to reconsider your gaming habits or seek advice.
