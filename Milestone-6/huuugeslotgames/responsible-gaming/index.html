Warning Signs

Gambling should be entertaining, not a way to make money. If you're concerned about your gaming habits, here are some warning signs to consider:

Spending More Than You Can Afford

Using money meant for essentials like bills or rent to play games, or spending more than you intended.

Neglecting Responsibilities

Missing work, school, or family obligations because of gaming, or declining social invitations to play.

Chasing Losses

Continuing to play to try to win back virtual coins you've lost, often leading to greater losses.

Hiding Your Gaming

Being secretive about how much time or money you spend gaming, or lying to others about it.

Mood Swings Related to Gaming

Feeling irritable, anxious, or depressed when not playing, or needing to play to feel good.

Difficulty Controlling Play

Repeated unsuccessful attempts to cut back or stop playing, or feeling restless when trying to reduce play.

If you recognize several of these signs in yourself or someone you care about, consider seeking help.
