Top 6 Common Curtain Mistakes

June 15, 2025 â€¢ 5 min read

Selecting and hanging curtains seems simple, but many homeowners make these common mistakes that can ruin the look of their window treatments. Avoid these errors for perfect curtains every time.

1. Choosing the Wrong Length

The most frequent mistake is selecting curtains that are too short. Curtains should either just touch the floor or have a slight puddle (1-3 inches) for an elegant look. Avoid the "high-water" effect that makes windows look undersized.

2. Incorrect Rod Placement

Hanging the rod right above the window frame makes the window appear smaller. Instead, mount the rod 4-6 inches above the frame and extend it 3-6 inches beyond the window on each side to create width.

3. Skimping on Fabric Width

Curtains should have 2 to 2.5 times the width of the window when closed to appear full and luxurious. Too little fabric looks skimpy and fails to block light effectively.

4. Ignoring Light Control Needs

Consider the room's purpose. Bedrooms need blackout curtains, while living areas may prefer light-filtering options. Don't choose based on looks alone.

5. Forgetting About Lining

Unlined curtains fade faster and offer poor insulation. Quality lining protects fabric, improves drape, and enhances light/heat control.

6. Wrong Style for the Space

Heavy drapes in a casual space or sheer panels in a formal room create visual discord. Match the curtain style to your room's aesthetic.
