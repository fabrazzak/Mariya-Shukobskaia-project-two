Best Walking Routes from Our Sites

Explore stunning Welsh landscapes with these recommended walks starting at our caravan parks, from coastal paths to mountain trails.

Walking Guide

Expert-curated trails

One of the greatest joys of caravanning in Wales is stepping straight from your pitch onto spectacular walking trails. Our sites are carefully chosen for their access to some of Wales' finest landscapes. Whether you prefer gentle coastal strolls or challenging mountain hikes, these routes offer something for every ability level.

1. Pembrokeshire Coast Path from St David's Lleithyr Meadow

This stunning coastal walk starts right from the site and showcases the best of the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park:

  • Distance: 5 miles (8km) circular route
  • Difficulty: Moderate with some steep sections
  • Highlights: Whitesands Bay, St David's Head, ancient standing stones
  • Time: 2.5-3 hours

The route begins with a gentle climb to St David's Head with panoramic views across Ramsey Island. Look out for seals and seabirds along the dramatic cliffs. The return leg passes prehistoric remains before descending to the golden sands of Whitesands Bay - perfect for a post-walk swim in summer.

"The Pembrokeshire Coast Path from St David's is our favorite walk in Wales. The views are breathtaking, and finishing at Whitesands Bay with an ice cream is the perfect reward." — Regular walkers

2. Snowdon Ranger Path from Coed-y-Llwyn

For those seeking a mountain challenge, this path up Snowdon starts just 15 minutes from the site:

  • Distance: 8 miles (13km) return
  • Difficulty: Challenging
  • Ascent: 936m (3,070ft)
  • Time: 5-6 hours

The Snowdon Ranger Path is one of the quieter routes up Wales' highest peak. It follows the original 18th-century tourist path with steady gradients and spectacular views over Llyn Cwellyn. The summit offers panoramic views across Snowdonia - on clear days you can see as far as Ireland.

3. Four Waterfalls Walk from Brecon Beacons

A magical walk through ancient woodland to four stunning waterfalls:

  • Distance: 5.5 miles (9km) circular
  • Difficulty: Moderate with some steep steps
  • Highlights: Sgwd Clun-Gwyn, Sgwd Isaf Clun-Gwyn, Sgwd y Pannwr, Sgwd yr Eira
  • Time: 3-4 hours

Starting from the site, this walk follows the River Mellte through beautiful woodland. The highlight is Sgwd yr Eira where you can walk behind the curtain of water (prepare to get misted!). Sturdy footwear is essential as paths can be slippery after rain.

4. Anglesey Coastal Path from Penrhos

A gentle section of the famous coastal path perfect for all ages:

  • Distance: 4 miles (6.5km) linear
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Red Wharf Bay, Benllech Beach, coastal wildlife
  • Time: 2 hours

This accessible walk follows sandy paths along the east Anglesey coast. Start at the site and walk to Red Wharf Bay where you can reward yourself with seafood at the Ship Inn. Return via the same route or catch the bus back from Benllech.

5. Wye Valley Walk from Pandy

A beautiful riverside walk with historic interest:

  • Distance: 6 miles (10km) circular
  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • Highlights: Llanthony Priory, Offa's Dyke, River Honddu
  • Time: 3 hours

From Pandy site, this route combines sections of the Wye Valley Walk and Offa's Dyke Path. The ruined Llanthony Priory makes a perfect picnic spot, while the return leg follows the rushing River Honddu through beautiful woodland.

Walking Tips and Essentials

To make the most of your walking adventures:

  1. Check the weather - Welsh conditions can change rapidly, especially in mountains
  2. Pack appropriately - Waterproofs, sturdy boots, map, compass, snacks and water
  3. Know your limits - Choose routes suitable for your group's fitness level
  4. Leave no trace - Take all litter home and respect wildlife
  5. Tell someone - Let site staff know your planned route and return time

Most of our sites have drying rooms - essential for getting boots and gear dry after a day on the trails. Many also loan walking poles and can recommend local guides for more challenging routes.

Why Walk from Your Caravan?

Starting walks directly from your caravan site offers many advantages:

  • No parking hassles - Begin and end your walk at your accommodation
  • Flexible timing - Set out when you're ready without transport schedules
  • Local knowledge - Site staff can recommend routes and conditions
  • Comfortable return - Hot showers and home comforts await
  • Eco-friendly - Reduce your carbon footprint by walking from base

Whether you're seeking challenging mountain ascents or gentle seaside strolls, Wales offers some of the UK's finest walking straight from our caravan sites. With dramatic landscapes, rich wildlife and well-maintained paths, these routes showcase the very best of the Welsh countryside.
