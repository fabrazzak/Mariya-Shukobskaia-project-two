Top 5 Coastal Caravan Sites in Wales

Discover the best seaside locations for your motorhome or caravan holiday in Wales, from the Gower Peninsula to Anglesey.

Coastal Destinations

Expert-curated guide

Wales boasts some of the most spectacular coastal scenery in the UK, with dramatic cliffs, golden sandy beaches, and picturesque harbours. What better way to experience these stunning locations than from one of Wales' premier coastal caravan and motorhome sites? After extensive research and first-hand visits, we've selected our top 5 coastal sites that offer exceptional facilities and breathtaking sea views.

1. Gowerton Caravan and Motorhome Club Site

Located in the Gower Peninsula, Wales' first Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, Gowerton offers direct access to some of the country's finest beaches. The site features:

  • 128 spacious pitches (both hardstanding and grass)
  • 16 amp electric hook-ups for all pitches
  • Modern toilet and shower facilities
  • Dog walking area and children's play space
  • 10-minute walk to Three Cliffs Bay

What makes Gowerton special is its proximity to Rhossili Bay, regularly voted one of the best beaches in Europe. The three-mile stretch of golden sand is perfect for swimming, surfing, or simply relaxing with a picnic while watching the sunset over Worm's Head.

"The views from the cliff-top pitches at Gowerton are simply breathtaking. Waking up to see the sun rising over the Bristol Channel was a highlight of our trip." — Regular visitor

2. St David's Lleithyr Meadow

Nestled between three dramatic headlands on the Pembrokeshire Coast, this site is a paradise for coastal walkers and water sports enthusiasts. Key features include:

  • 115 grass pitches (some seasonal)
  • All pitches with 16 amp electric hook-up
  • Easy access to the Pembrokeshire Coastal Path
  • 1.5 miles from Whitesands Bay (perfect for surfing)
  • On-site information point with local walking guides

The site's location makes it ideal for exploring St David's, Britain's smallest city, with its magnificent cathedral and charming local shops. Boat trips to the nearby islands of Ramsey, Skomer, and Skokholm (famous for their puffin colonies) are highly recommended.

3. Penrhos Caravan and Motorhome Club Site

On the beautiful Isle of Anglesey, Penrhos offers a peaceful retreat with easy access to some of North Wales' best beaches. Site highlights:

  • 91 pitches (74 hardstanding, 17 grass)
  • Two miles from Benllech beach (Blue Flag status)
  • Heated indoor swimming pool (seasonal)
  • Adventure playground for children
  • Excellent public transport links

The site is particularly well-suited for families, with the shallow waters of Benllech Bay being ideal for young children. Don't miss the coastal walk to nearby Red Wharf Bay, with its excellent seafood restaurants.

4. Freshwater East Caravan and Motorhome Club Site

Located within the Pembrokeshire Coast National Park, Freshwater East offers a perfect blend of coastal and countryside views. What to expect:

  • 142 pitches (77 hardstanding)
  • Tiered pitch areas with sea views
  • Five-minute walk to the beach
  • Excellent birdwatching opportunities
  • Close to historic Pembroke Castle

The beach at Freshwater East is a hidden gem - a wide, sandy bay sheltered by cliffs, perfect for swimming and sandcastle building. The site is also well-placed for exploring the historic towns of Tenby and Saundersfoot.

5. Pembrey Country Park Caravan and Motorhome Club Site

Set on the edge of a 520-acre country park in Carmarthenshire, this site offers both woodland and coastal experiences. Notable features:

  • 125 touring pitches (69 hardstanding)
  • Access to seven miles of sandy beach
  • On-site cycle hire
  • Dry ski slope and toboggan run in the country park
  • Excellent disabled facilities

Pembrey's beach is one of the longest in Wales, with dunes that create a microclimate perfect for sunbathing. The site is particularly good for active families, with numerous walking and cycling trails through the park.

Planning Your Coastal Caravan Holiday

When booking a coastal caravan site in Wales, consider these tips:

  1. Book early - Coastal sites are popular, especially during school holidays
  2. Check pitch types - Some coastal sites can be exposed to sea breezes, so you might prefer a sheltered hardstanding pitch
  3. Pack for all weathers - Even in summer, coastal areas can be cooler than inland
  4. Research tide times - Essential for beach activities and coastal walks
  5. Consider a seasonal pitch - If you love coastal caravanning, some sites offer seasonal pitches at reduced rates

All the sites mentioned are members of the Caravan and Motorhome Club, offering high standards of facilities and customer service. Non-members can usually join on arrival to take advantage of member rates.

Whichever coastal site you choose, you're guaranteed stunning sea views, fresh sea air, and unforgettable memories of Wales' spectacular coastline.
