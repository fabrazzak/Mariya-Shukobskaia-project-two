Healthy Gaming Habits

Tips for enjoying Tennis Club Story in a balanced way

Set Time Limits

Use your device's screen time features to set reasonable play sessions. Take regular breaks every hour to rest your eyes and stretch.

Balance Activities

Make sure gaming is just one of many activities in your daily routine. Maintain time for work, study, exercise, and social connections.

Play Socially

Share your gaming experiences with friends or family. Discussing strategies and achievements can make gaming more social and less isolating.

Monitor Your Mood

Be aware of how gaming affects your emotions. If you feel frustrated, anxious, or irritable when playing, it may be time to take a break.

Spend Wisely

If making in-game purchases, set a monthly budget and stick to it. Never spend money you can't afford to lose.

Prioritize Sleep

Avoid playing right before bedtime. The blue light from screens can disrupt sleep patterns, affecting your health and well-being.

Warning Signs

Be aware of these signs that gaming may be becoming a problem

  • Spending increasing amounts of time gaming, to the point where other areas of life are neglected
  • Becoming irritable, anxious, or angry when unable to play
  • Lying to family or friends about time spent gaming
  • Using gaming to escape from real-life problems or negative feelings
  • Continuing to play despite negative consequences to work, school, or relationships
  • Needing to spend more money on gaming to get the same level of enjoyment

Parental Controls

Tools to help manage your child's gaming experience

Managing Your Child's Play

Tennis Club Story is rated for ages 3+ and contains no inappropriate content. However, we recommend parents:

  • Set time limits using your device's parental controls
  • Play together with younger children to make it a shared activity
  • Discuss in-game purchases and set spending limits if applicable
  • Encourage breaks for physical activity and other hobbies
