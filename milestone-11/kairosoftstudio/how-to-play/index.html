Getting Started

Learn the basics of managing your tennis club and training players

1

Set Up Your Club

Begin by naming your tennis club and choosing its location. This will determine your starting facilities and potential player base.

2

Recruit Players

Scout and recruit talented players to join your club. Look for athletes with good stats in power, technique, and stamina.

3

Design Training

Create custom training programs focusing on different skills. Balance power and technique training based on your players' needs.

4

Enter Tournaments

Register your players for singles, doubles, and team competitions. Winning tournaments increases your club's prestige.

5

Attract Sponsors

As your club gains fame, sponsors will offer funding and equipment. Choose sponsors that match your club's goals.

6

Upgrade Facilities

Use sponsor funds to upgrade your courts, build new facilities like spas or restaurants, and attract more members.

Advanced Tips

Pro strategies to take your tennis club to the next level

Finding the right balance between power and technique training is crucial:

  • Power training increases serve speed and shot strength
  • Technique training improves accuracy and control
  • Younger players benefit more from balanced training
  • Specialize veteran players based on their natural strengths

Pro Tip: Monitor player fatigue and schedule rest days to prevent injuries that can sideline your stars during important tournaments.

Sponsors can make or break your club's financial success:

  • Local businesses offer steady income with modest requirements
  • Corporate sponsors provide big money but demand tournament wins
  • Equipment sponsors reduce your training costs
  • Facility sponsors can build restaurants or spas that boost player morale

Pro Tip: Don't overcommit to sponsor requirements early in the game. Build your team first, then pursue lucrative sponsors when you can consistently win tournaments.

Winning tournaments is key to growing your club's reputation:

  • Start with local tournaments to build confidence
  • Schedule tournaments based on your players' condition
  • Enter doubles tournaments to develop teamwork
  • Grand Slam events offer the biggest prestige boosts

Pro Tip: Before major tournaments, host special training camps to boost your players' stats. The temporary boost can make the difference in close matches.

Developing star players is essential for long-term success:

  • Young players have higher potential but take longer to develop
  • Veteran players can contribute immediately but plateau sooner
  • Pay attention to player personalities - some respond better to intense training
  • Rotate players to keep them fresh and avoid burnout

Pro Tip: Identify one or two players with exceptional potential early and focus your best training resources on them. These future stars will anchor your team for years.

Hosting events keeps your members engaged and attracts new players:

  • Barbecues boost morale for all players
  • Training camps provide temporary stat boosts
  • Exhibition matches increase club visibility
  • Special events unlock unique rewards

Pro Tip: Schedule events before important tournaments or when player morale is low. The timing can significantly impact your team's performance.
