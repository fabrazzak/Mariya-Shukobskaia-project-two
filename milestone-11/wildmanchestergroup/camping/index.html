Why Choose Wild Camping?

Escape the crowds and experience nature in its purest form with our wild camping adventures.

True Wilderness Experience

Get away from crowded campsites and enjoy the solitude of nature. Our carefully selected locations offer peace, quiet, and stunning natural beauty.

Essential Skills Training

Learn how to set up camp responsibly, build safe fires, navigate terrain, and leave no trace. Our guides will teach you everything you need to know.

Wildlife Encounters

Experience Manchester's wildlife in their natural habitat. From deer to owls, our camping spots offer incredible opportunities for wildlife observation.

Our Camping Experiences

Whether you're a first-time camper or a seasoned outdoors enthusiast, we have the perfect adventure for you.

Beginner Wild Camping

Perfect for your first wild camping experience. We provide all the equipment and guidance you need to feel comfortable and safe in the wilderness.

  • 1-night introduction to wild camping
  • All equipment provided (tent, sleeping bag, mat)
  • Small group (max 6 people)
  • Basic skills training included

Intermediate Wild Camping

For those with some camping experience who want to take their skills further. More remote locations and additional challenges.

  • 2-night adventure in more remote locations
  • Bring your own equipment (or rent ours)
  • Advanced skills training (navigation, weather reading)
  • Small group (max 8 people)

Family Wild Camping

Designed for families with children aged 7+. A safe and fun introduction to wild camping for the whole family.

  • 1-night camping adventure
  • All equipment provided
  • Nature activities for kids
  • Campfire stories and marshmallow toasting

Survival Camping

For those who want to test their limits. Minimal equipment, maximum challenge under expert supervision.

  • 3-day survival experience
  • Learn shelter-building, fire-making, foraging
  • Small group (max 6 people)
  • Must complete beginner course first

What to Expect on Your Trip

Our wild camping adventures follow a carefully designed itinerary to ensure you have an unforgettable experience while staying safe.

1

Afternoon: Meet & Gear Check

We'll meet at our base camp, go through equipment, and discuss safety procedures before heading out to our camping location.

2

Evening: Set Up Camp

Arrive at our carefully selected camping spot and learn how to set up your tent responsibly in the wilderness.

3

Night: Campfire & Stories

Enjoy a campfire meal (where permitted), learn about the night sky, and share stories around the fire.

4

Morning: Sunrise & Pack Up

Wake with the dawn, enjoy breakfast in nature, and learn how to leave no trace as we pack up camp.

Essential Camping Gear

We provide all necessary equipment for beginners, but here's what you should bring for intermediate and advanced trips.

Shelter

  • Lightweight tent or tarp
  • Sleeping bag (appropriate rating)
  • Sleeping pad or mat

Cooking

  • Portable camping stove
  • Cooking pot and utensils
  • Water bottle or hydration pack

Clothing

  • Moisture-wicking base layers
  • Insulating mid layers
  • Waterproof outer shell

Safety

  • First aid kit
  • Headlamp or flashlight
  • Map and compass (GPS optional)

Camping FAQs

Answers to common questions about our wild camping adventures.

Wild camping in England generally requires the landowner's permission. All our camping locations are carefully selected with proper permissions arranged in advance. We strictly follow the Countryside Code and Leave No Trace principles.

Our beginner camping trips are designed specifically for first-timers. We provide all the equipment you'll need and our expert guides will teach you everything from setting up your tent to cooking outdoors. You'll be in good hands!

Part of the wild camping experience is learning to manage without modern facilities. We'll teach you how to handle personal hygiene responsibly in the wilderness. For beginner trips, we choose locations with some basic facilities nearby.

We monitor weather closely and will reschedule if severe conditions are forecast. For light rain (common in Manchester!), we proceed with extra preparation. You'll learn how to stay dry and comfortable in various conditions.

On select trips, well-behaved dogs are welcome. They must be kept under control at all times due to wildlife and livestock. Please contact us in advance if you plan to bring your dog.
