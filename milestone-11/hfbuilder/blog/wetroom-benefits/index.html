The Benefits of Converting to a Wetroom

March 22, 2025 • 5 min read

Wetrooms have surged in popularity across Glasgow homes, offering a sleek, modern alternative to traditional bathrooms. These fully waterproofed spaces eliminate shower trays and curtains, creating a seamless, open-plan bathing area. At House Force, we've seen firsthand how wetroom conversions can transform both the functionality and value of a property.

1. Space Optimization

One of the most significant advantages of wetrooms is their space-saving design:

  • No shower enclosure: Eliminates bulky shower trays and screens
  • Visual expansion: Creates the illusion of more space with continuous flooring
  • Flexible layouts: Can be installed in rooms as small as 1.5m x 1.5m
  • Underfloor heating: Often incorporated, eliminating the need for radiators

Ideal for Awkward Spaces

Wetrooms are particularly beneficial for:

  • Loft conversions with sloping ceilings
  • Under-stair bathrooms
  • En-suite additions to small bedrooms
  • Basement conversions

2. Enhanced Accessibility

Wetrooms provide superior accessibility compared to traditional bathrooms:

Feature Traditional Bathroom Wetroom
Floor Level Step into shower/bath Fully level access
Mobility Aid Friendly Limited space Wheelchair accessible
Safety Potential slip hazards Non-slip flooring throughout
Future-Proofing May need adaptation Ready for aging in place
"For clients planning to age in place or those with mobility challenges, wetrooms offer a stylish solution that won't need future modification. The level access and open design provide safety without compromising on aesthetics."

3. Increased Property Value

Quality wetroom installations can significantly boost your home's market appeal:

  • Premium feature: Viewed as a luxury addition by buyers
  • ROI: Typically recoup 70-80% of installation costs
  • Universal design: Appeals to all demographics
  • Modern appeal: Makes older properties more contemporary

4. Simplified Cleaning & Maintenance

The streamlined design of wetrooms reduces cleaning time and maintenance:

No More Mold & Mildew

Traditional shower enclosures create perfect environments for mold growth. Wetrooms eliminate:

  • Shower curtains that harbor bacteria
  • Sealant lines that degrade over time
  • Corners where grime accumulates

Durable Materials

Wetrooms use:

  • Large-format tiles with minimal grout lines
  • Non-porous surfaces that resist staining
  • Waterproof tanking systems behind walls

5. Design Flexibility

Wetrooms offer unparalleled creative possibilities:

Material Choices

  • Flooring: From textured stone to heated porcelain
  • Walls: Glass, tile, or waterproof plaster
  • Features: Built-in niches, seating, and shelving

Style Options

6. Waterproofing Technology

Modern tanking systems make wetrooms more reliable than ever:

  • Liquid membranes: Brush or roll-on waterproof coatings
  • Sheet membranes: Peel-and-stick waterproof barriers
  • Pre-formed trays: Gradient bases for proper drainage
  • Drainage systems: Linear or point drains with sleek covers

7. Ideal for Rental Properties

Landlords are increasingly choosing wetrooms for rental units because:

  • Durability: Withstands heavy use better than conventional bathrooms
  • Lower maintenance costs: Fewer repairs needed between tenancies
  • Universal appeal: Attracts diverse tenant demographics
  • Higher perceived value: Can command increased rents

8. Health & Wellness Benefits

Beyond practicality, wetrooms enhance daily living:

Therapeutic Features

  • Steam showers: Easily incorporated into wetroom designs
  • Rainfall showers: No enclosure limits showerhead size
  • Hydrotherapy: Space for multiple body jets

Improved Air Quality

Better ventilation reduces:

  • Humidity levels that cause mold spores
  • Musty odors from damp towels and curtains
  • Condensation on mirrors and surfaces

9. Cost Considerations

While wetrooms have higher upfront costs than standard bathrooms, they offer long-term value:

Component Average Cost Notes
Waterproofing £800-£1,500 Most critical part of installation
Flooring £40-£100/m² Non-slip tiles recommended
Drainage £150-£400 Linear drains cost more but look sleeker
Labor £3,000-£6,000 Depends on size and complexity

Is a Wetroom Right for Your Glasgow Home?

Wetrooms offer compelling benefits but require professional installation to prevent water damage. Consider a wetroom conversion if you:

  • Want to maximize a small bathroom space
  • Need future-proof accessibility features
  • Prefer sleek, modern design aesthetics
  • Value easy-to-clean surfaces
  • Are renovating an awkwardly shaped space
