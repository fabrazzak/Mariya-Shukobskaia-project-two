The Tech Behind Our Water Physics

Published on March 10, 2025 • 7 min read • By JustCool Engineering Team

Creating realistic water physics for Boat Attack was one of our biggest technical challenges. Today we're pulling back the curtain to share how we achieved fluid, responsive water interactions that work across a wide range of mobile devices while maintaining stunning visual quality.

The Challenge

Water simulation in games typically requires significant computational resources. Our goals for Boat Attack presented unique challenges:

  • Real-time interaction between boats and water surface
  • Dynamic wave propagation that affects gameplay
  • Visual fidelity that holds up on mobile screens
  • Consistent 60fps performance on mid-range devices
  • Believable physics for boats of different sizes and weights
Performance Constraints

Mobile GPUs have strict limits on vertex processing and fill rates. Our water system needed to work within these constraints while still delivering visually impressive results.

Our Solution

After months of experimentation, we developed a hybrid approach combining multiple techniques:

1. Vertex Displacement Shader

The foundation of our water system is a vertex displacement shader that modifies the water mesh in real-time:

// Simplified vertex shader excerpt
void vert(inout appdata_full v) {
  // Sample wave texture
  float wave = tex2Dlod(_WaveTex, float4(v.texcoord.xy, 0, 0)).r;
  // Apply displacement
  v.vertex.y += wave * _WaveHeight;
  // Calculate normals for lighting
  v.normal = normalize(float3(
    -ddx(v.vertex.y),
    1.0,
    -ddy(v.vertex.y)
  ));
}

2. Physics Interaction System

Boats interact with the water surface through a custom physics system:

  • Each boat has multiple buoyancy points sampling water height
  • Forces are calculated based on submersion depth and velocity
  • Wave reactions are approximated using impulse responses

3. Optimized Wave Propagation

Rather than simulating full fluid dynamics (too expensive for mobile), we use:

Procedural Detail

To add visual detail without heavy computation, we use screen-space reflections and procedural foam that generates based on wave steepness and boat interactions.

Performance Optimization

Making this all work on mobile required several optimizations:

  • Level of Detail (LOD): Water mesh complexity reduces with distance
  • Approximate Calculations: Using half-precision floats where possible
  • Baking: Pre-computing static wave patterns
  • Shader Variants: Different quality levels for different devices

The Results

Our final implementation achieves:

  • 60fps on devices as old as iPhone X
  • Responsive physics that affects gameplay
  • Visually impressive waves and interactions
  • Only 2-3ms of GPU time per frame
"The water physics became a defining feature of Boat Attack - players consistently mention how good it feels to control the boats through waves."

Future Improvements

We're already working on enhancements for future updates:

  • Better wave interactions between multiple boats
  • More detailed foam and spray effects
  • Dynamic water trails that persist longer
  • Improved reflections on high-end devices
