Game Guide

Top 10 Survival Tips for New Players

Tigran Sargsyan

Posted on April 15, 2025 • 5 min read

Just stranded on the island? These essential tips will help you survive your first week and establish a secure base in Survival Island: Dinosaurs Pro. Whether you're a complete beginner or looking to refine your survival strategy, these tips will give you the edge you need.

1. Prioritize Basic Tools

Your first minutes on the island are crucial. Focus on gathering these essentials:

Stone Axe

The most versatile starter tool for gathering wood and basic defense.

  • • Gathers wood faster than by hand
  • • Can be used as a weak weapon
  • • Requires only stone and sticks

Stone Pickaxe

Essential for mining rocks and gathering minerals.

  • • Mines stone 3x faster than axe
  • • Required for flint gathering
  • • Upgrades to metal tools later

2. Secure Fresh Water Immediately

Dehydration kills faster than hunger in Survival Island. Follow these steps:

  • • Locate the nearest freshwater source (rivers are safest)
  • • Craft a water skin as soon as possible (requires 3 leather)
  • • Boil water before drinking to avoid illness (craft a campfire)
  • • Coconuts provide temporary hydration in emergencies

Pro Tip

Build your first shelter near (but not too close to) a water source. About 50-100 meters away provides safety from predators that frequent watering holes while keeping hydration accessible.

3. Build a Shelter Before Nightfall

Your first night is the most dangerous. Follow this shelter progression:

Shelter Type Materials Needed Protection Level
Lean-to 20 wood, 10 leaves Basic (weather only)
Wooden Hut 50 wood, 20 stone Moderate (small predators)
Stone Shelter 100 stone, 30 wood High (most dinosaurs)

4. Learn Dinosaur Behavior Patterns

Understanding dinosaur AI is key to survival:

  • • Herbivores are generally passive unless provoked
  • • Most predators hunt at dawn and dusk
  • • Running through water can break line of sight
  • • Some species are territorial near nests

5. Establish a Food Source

Reliable food prevents starvation:

Foraging

Berries, mushrooms, coconuts (quick but limited)

Hunting

Small game, fish (requires tools/skill)

Farming

Long-term solution (takes time to establish)

6. Craft Storage Early

Inventory management is crucial. Prioritize these containers:

  1. Basic Storage Box (20 slots) - 30 wood
  2. Preservation Bin (food lasts 2x longer) - 40 wood, 10 stone
  3. Water Tank (stores 5 water skins) - 50 wood, 20 thatch

7. Explore Carefully

Follow these exploration rules:

Exploration Checklist


1. Always carry: 
   - 2 bandages 
   - 1 weapon 
   - 1 torch 
   - Emergency food/water

2. Mark your path with temporary markers

3. Don't explore at night until well-equipped

4. Return before your inventory is full

8. Learn Basic First Aid

Essential medical recipes to memorize:

  • • Bandage (2 cloth) - stops bleeding
  • • Herbal Poultice (3 medicinal herbs) - reduces infection risk
  • • Splint (4 sticks, 2 cloth) - fixes broken bones

9. Upgrade Your Tools Strategically

Tool progression path for efficiency:

Tool Progression
Stone
Flint
Copper
Iron

10. Join the Community

Take advantage of community resources:

  • • Official wiki has complete crafting recipes
  • • Discord community shares strategies
  • • YouTube tutorials for advanced techniques

Remember, everyone starts as a beginner. With these tips and some practice, you'll be thriving on the island in no time. The most important survival tool is your knowledge!

- Tigran Sargsyan, Community Manager
