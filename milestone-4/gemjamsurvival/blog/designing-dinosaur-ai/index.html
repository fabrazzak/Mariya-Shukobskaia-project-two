Behind the Scenes: Designing Dinosaur AI for Survival Island

Hayk Harutyunyan

Posted on April 28, 2025 • 6 min read

Creating believable dinosaur behaviors that challenge players without feeling unfair was one of our biggest challenges in developing Survival Island. In this post, we'll take you through our AI systems and balancing process.

The Foundation: Behavior Trees

We started with a robust behavior tree system that allows us to create complex, hierarchical decision-making processes for each dinosaur species:

Core Behaviors

Basic survival instincts that all dinosaurs share:

  • • Hunger and hunting patterns
  • • Territorial defense mechanisms
  • • Environmental awareness

Species-Specific Traits

Unique characteristics for each dinosaur type:

  • • Pack hunting behaviors for raptors
  • • Defensive herding for herbivores
  • • Ambush tactics for large predators

Balancing Challenge vs. Fairness

One of our biggest challenges was making dinosaurs threatening without making them feel unfair. We implemented several systems to achieve this:

  • • Visual and audio cues before attacks
  • • Predictable attack patterns with tells
  • • Progressive aggression based on player actions
  • • Clear indicators of dinosaur awareness states

Design Insight

We found that players responded best to dinosaur AI that followed consistent rules they could learn. Randomness in behavior often felt unfair, while predictable patterns with some variation created the best gameplay experience.

The Sensory System

Dinosaurs in Survival Island use a multi-layered sensory system to detect players:

Sense Implementation
Vision Cone-based detection affected by lighting, movement, and camouflage
Hearing Sound propagation system with volume thresholds
Smell Scent trails from food and blood that decay over time
Vibration Footstep detection for nearby large creatures

Debugging & Optimization

Developing complex AI systems came with its share of challenges:

  • • Early versions had dinosaurs with "super senses" that spotted players too easily
  • • Pathfinding issues around complex terrain
  • • Memory leaks from behavior tree recursion
  • • Performance hits from too many active AI agents

AI Debug Visualization


// Example of our debug visualization system
public class DinosaurAIDebug : MonoBehaviour {
    void OnDrawGizmos() {
        if (!showDebug) return;
        
        // Draw vision cone
        Gizmos.color = Color.yellow;
        DrawVisionCone(transform.position, transform.forward, visionAngle, visionRange);
        
        // Draw current state
        GUI.Label(new Rect(10, 10, 200, 20), 
                "State: " + currentState.ToString());
        
        // Draw awareness level
        Gizmos.color = Color.Lerp(Color.green, Color.red, awareness);
        Gizmos.DrawWireSphere(transform.position, 2f);
    }
}

What We Learned

After months of iteration and player testing, we discovered some key insights about dinosaur AI:

  • • Players prefer AI that follows understandable rules over "realistic" unpredictability
  • • Visual feedback is crucial for player understanding of AI states
  • • Balancing requires different approaches for casual vs. hardcore players

Creating believable dinosaur behaviors was one of our most challenging but rewarding tasks. We're continuing to refine the AI systems based on player feedback and data.
