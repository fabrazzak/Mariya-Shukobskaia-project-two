Problem Gambling Warning Signs

Recognizing the signs of problem gambling is the first step to getting help.

Financial Warning Signs

  • Borrowing money to play games
  • Selling possessions to fund gameplay
  • Lying about spending on games
  • Neglecting bills to make in-app purchases

Emotional Warning Signs

  • Feeling restless or irritable when not playing
  • Using games to escape problems or relieve stress
  • Lying to conceal time or money spent
  • Feeling guilt or remorse after playing

Behavioral Warning Signs

  • Spending more time or money than intended
  • Neglecting work, school or family obligations
  • Failed attempts to cut back or stop
  • Chasing losses by continuing to play

Relationship Warning Signs

  • Arguments with family about gaming habits
  • Losing interest in other activities
  • Isolating from friends and family
  • Neglecting personal relationships

Tips for Responsible Gaming

Decide how much time you want to spend playing and stick to it. Use phone alarms or app timers to remind you when your session should end. Take regular breaks to maintain perspective.

If you choose to make in-app purchases, set a strict budget and never exceed it. Treat gaming expenses like entertainment costs, not investments. Most mobile devices allow you to set spending limits.

Make sure gaming doesn't replace other important activities like exercise, socializing, work, or family time. Maintain a healthy balance between gaming and other aspects of your life.

Remember that social casino games are for entertainment only. There's no way to "win" real money, so never play with the expectation of financial gain.

If you find yourself getting frustrated, angry, or upset while playing, it's time to take a break. Gaming should be enjoyable, not stressful.
