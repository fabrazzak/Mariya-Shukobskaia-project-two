What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website or use an app. They help the website or app remember information about your visit, which can make your next visit easier and more useful to you.

Managing Cookies

You can control and/or delete cookies as you wish. Most web browsers allow some control of most cookies through the browser settings. Here's how you can manage cookies:

Browser Settings

You can usually find cookie settings in the "Options" or "Preferences" menu of your browser. Common locations:

  • • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data

Mobile Devices

On mobile devices, cookie management may vary by operating system and browser:

  • • iOS: Settings > Safari > Privacy & Security
  • • Android: Browser settings > Privacy

Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of certain types of cookies:

  • • Google Analytics Opt-out
  • • Your Online Choices (for advertising cookies)

Local Storage

In addition to cookies, we may use local storage (HTML5) to store content information and preferences. Local storage is similar to cookies but can store larger amounts of data. You can clear local storage by clearing your browser's cache.
