Reflections on Our First Year in Sydney

A personal journey of bringing Korean café culture to Australia

Written by

Min-jun Kim

Co-founder & Head Barista

Our opening day in March 2022 - nervous but excited to share our vision

When we moved from Seoul to Sydney in 2021 with dreams of opening a Korean café, we had no idea how much we'd learn in that first whirlwind year. This is our honest reflection on the challenges, surprises, and beautiful moments that made our first year at Gami Caffè unforgettable.

The Vision: Why We Started

Min-jun:

After working in Seoul's specialty coffee scene for 10 years, I noticed how Korean cafés had developed their own unique identity - not quite Western, not traditional Korean, but a fascinating fusion. I wanted to bring that third-wave coffee approach with Korean flavors to Sydney, where I felt the café scene was ready for something new.

Soo-ah:

For me, it was about sharing the hospitality of Korean "jeong" (정) - that warm, familial connection you feel in neighborhood cafés back home. I wanted to recreate that feeling through desserts that tell stories, using recipes passed down from my grandmother but with modern techniques I learned at pastry school.

Our first sketch of the café concept from 2020

Key Milestones

Feb 2021

Securing Our Location

After 6 months searching, we found our George Street spot. The landlord took a chance on two first-time business owners with an unconventional concept.

May 2021

Menu Development

Two months of recipe testing - balancing authentic flavors with local Australian tastes. Our biggest challenge? Creating bingsu without a professional ice shaver!

Oct 2021

Construction Delays

COVID supply chain issues pushed our opening back by 3 months. We survived on instant noodles while paying rent on an unopened business.

Mar 2022

Grand Opening

After countless sleepless nights, we opened our doors to a line around the block. That first dalgona coffee we served still feels magical.

5 Lessons We Learned

1

Authenticity Over Imitation

Early on, we tried adapting our menu to what we thought Australians wanted. Our breakthrough came when we fully embraced Korean flavors - that's when customers started lining up.

2

The Korean-Australian Community is Everything

Word-of-mouth in the Korean community gave us our first regulars. Their feedback helped us perfect recipes and create menu items like our kimchi grilled cheese.

3

Staff Are Family

Our first hire, Ji-soo, became like a younger sister. Investing in staff training and wellbeing created the warm atmosphere we're known for.

4

Small Details Make Big Differences

From the specific sound of our door chime to how we fold napkins - these subtle touches create the full experience customers remember.

Our Favorite Customer Moments

The Birthday Regular

Mrs. Kim, a 78-year-old Korean grandmother, came every Tuesday for our injeolmi bingsu. When we surprised her with a birthday version decorated with edible flowers, she cried and hugged us.

The Proposal

James proposed to Sarah at our corner table with a custom bingsu we helped design - strawberry with a ring-shaped white chocolate decoration. They still visit on their anniversary.

The Kids' Drawings

Two sisters who came every weekend after Korean school started leaving us drawings. We now have a rotating gallery of their artwork behind the counter.

Looking Ahead

Sustainability Goals

Transitioning to 100% compostable packaging and implementing a coffee grounds recycling program by end of 2023.

Recipe Book

Working on a cookbook sharing our modern takes on traditional Korean desserts, due for release next year.

Possible Expansion

Exploring a second location in Chatswood to better serve Sydney's North Shore community.

Final Thoughts

Min-jun:

"This year taught me that good coffee can bridge cultures. When I see Australian construction workers ordering dalgona alongside Korean students, I know we're creating something special."

Soo-ah:

"Every exhausted night was worth it for the moments when customers tell us our café feels like a taste of home - whether that home is Seoul or Sydney."

To everyone who supported us this year - our staff, customers, suppliers, and fellow small business owners in the George Street community - 감사합니다 (thank you). Here's to many more years of sharing Korean warmth, one cup of coffee at a time.
