How to Make Authentic Korean Bingsu at Home

Our step-by-step guide to creating the perfect shaved ice dessert with authentic flavors

Written by

Min-jun Kim

Head Barista & Co-founder at Gami Caffè

Homemade injeolmi bingsu with roasted soybean powder and rice cakes

Bingsu (빙수), Korea's beloved shaved ice dessert, might seem intimidating to make at home, but with the right techniques and ingredients, you can recreate authentic flavors in your own kitchen. As someone who grew up enjoying bingsu in Seoul's cafés, I'll share professional tips we use at Gami Caffè adapted for home cooks.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare the Ice

Option 1 (Blender method): Freeze milk in ice cube tray for 4 hours. Blend frozen milk cubes with regular ice cubes in a high-powered blender in short pulses until you achieve a snow-like consistency. Be careful not to overblend into a slush.

Option 2 (Traditional method): If you have time, freeze a block of sweetened milk (mix 2 cups milk with 2 tbsp sugar) overnight. Scrape with a sturdy spoon or ice shaver for fluffier texture.

2

Build the Base

Place shaved ice in a wide bowl, creating a slight well in the center. Lightly drizzle 1 tbsp condensed milk over the ice to help the powder adhere.

3

Add Flavor Layers

Sprinkle half the soybean powder evenly over the ice. Add half the rice cakes. Drizzle with another tbsp of condensed milk. Repeat layers, finishing with a dusting of pure soybean flour for visual appeal.

4

Final Touches

If using, drizzle honey or brown sugar syrup around the edges. Serve immediately with a long spoon for proper mixing.

Pro Tips from Our Kitchen

Texture is key: The ideal bingsu has flakes about 2-3mm thick. If using a blender, pulse in 2-second bursts and check frequently.

Temperature matters: Serve at -5°C to -8°C for perfect consistency. Warmer and it melts too fast, colder and it's too hard.

Rice cake prep: Soak store-bought injeolmi in warm water for 10 minutes to soften if they seem too hard.

Variations to Try

Strawberry Bingsu

Replace soybean powder with strawberry puree and fresh strawberries. Top with strawberry ice cream.

Matcha Red Bean

Use matcha powder and sweet red bean paste (pat) as primary flavors with mochi pieces.

Mango Cheese

Fresh mango cubes with cream cheese foam and condensed milk drizzle.

Recipe Notes

Storage: Bingsu doesn't store well - make only what you'll eat immediately. You can prepare toppings in advance.

Vegan option: Use coconut milk ice cubes and maple syrup instead of condensed milk.

Where to buy ingredients: Korean grocery stores carry injeolmi-garu and rice cakes. Look for brands with no additives.
