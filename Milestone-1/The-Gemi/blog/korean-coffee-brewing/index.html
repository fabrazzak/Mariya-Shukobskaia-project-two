Korean Coffee Brewing Methods You Should Try

From traditional hand-drip to viral dalgona, explore Korea's unique coffee culture

Written by

Min-jun Kim

Head Barista & Co-founder at Gami Caffè

Traditional and modern Korean coffee preparation techniques

While Korea might not grow coffee beans, its café culture has developed unique brewing methods that create distinct flavor profiles. After working in Seoul's specialty coffee scene for a decade, I've curated the most interesting Korean techniques that home brewers can experiment with.

1. Hand-Drip Coffee (Handeulipeu)

Korean hand-drip coffee takes the Japanese pour-over method and adds ceremonial elements from tea culture. The process is meditative and produces a clean, bright cup.

Key Characteristics:

  • Uses medium-coarse grind (slightly finer than Western pour-over)
  • Water temperature precisely controlled at 88-92°C
  • Ceremonial pouring technique with precise spiral motions
  • Typically brewed directly into glass cups for visual appreciation

How It Differs:

Korean baristas often use a "double filter" method with both paper and metal filters for extra clarity. The pour is slower with more attention to water distribution.

Try This at Home:

Use a gooseneck kettle to control your pour. Start with 15g coffee to 250ml water. Pour in 50ml increments, waiting 30 seconds between pours. The entire process should take about 3.5 minutes.

2. Dalgona Whipped Coffee

Made famous during the pandemic, this whipped coffee drink actually has roots in Korean "ppopgi" (sugar candy) traditions. The fluffy coffee topping sits atop cold milk.

Traditional vs. Modern:

Traditional Modern
Sweetener Brown sugar White sugar
Method Whisked by hand Electric mixer
Serving Hot milk Iced milk
Coffee Instant only Espresso possible

Authentic Dalgona Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp instant coffee
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp hot water
  • 200ml cold milk
  • Ice cubes (optional)

Steps:

  1. Combine coffee, sugar and water in a bowl
  2. Whisk vigorously for 5-7 minutes until thick and creamy
  3. Fill glass with milk (and ice if desired)
  4. Spoon whipped coffee on top
  5. Mix before drinking
