Environmental Stewardship

The Northern Pipeline Expansion incorporates numerous environmental protection measures:

  • Reclaimed all temporary workspace in completed sections (42 km to date)
  • Protected 14 identified wildlife corridors with specialized crossing structures
  • Implemented real-time water quality monitoring at all watercourse crossings
  • Planted over 8,000 native trees and shrubs in reclaimed areas

"The environmental protections on this project have set a new benchmark for how pipeline construction can coexist with sensitive ecosystems. Strike Group's team has been exemplary in implementing these measures."

- Dr. Emily Chen, Independent Environmental Monitor

Challenges Overcome

The project has faced and overcome several significant challenges:

March 2025

Record Snowpack

Unusually heavy winter snowfall delayed access to Section 3B. Our team implemented accelerated snow melt techniques to regain schedule.

April 2025

Wetland Protection

An unexpected archaeological find in a wetland area required redesign of 1.2 km of route. We worked with regulators to develop an acceptable solution within 3 weeks.

May 2025

Supply Chain Disruption

A delay in valve deliveries threatened to impact the schedule. We sourced alternative suppliers and implemented temporary bypass solutions.
