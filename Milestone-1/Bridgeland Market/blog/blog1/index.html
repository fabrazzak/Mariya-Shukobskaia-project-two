The Art of Cheese and Wine Pairing

Discover how to create perfect pairings with our guide to matching artisanal cheeses with complementary wines for your next gathering.

By Jane Doe Cheese Specialist
A beautifully arranged cheese and wine pairing board featuring selections from our shop

Creating the perfect cheese and wine pairing is an art form that can elevate your entertaining to new heights. At Bridgeland Market Gifts, we've spent years perfecting these combinations to help our customers create memorable experiences. Whether you're hosting a dinner party or enjoying a quiet evening, these pairings will delight your palate.

The Basic Principles of Pairing

Before we dive into specific pairings, let's cover the fundamental principles that guide successful cheese and wine combinations:

  • Match intensity: Bold cheeses need bold wines, while delicate cheeses pair better with lighter wines.
  • Complement or contrast: Either match similar flavors or create pleasant contrasts (like sweet wine with salty cheese).
  • Consider texture: Creamy cheeses often pair well with crisp, acidic wines that cut through the richness.
  • Regional pairings: Cheeses and wines from the same region often complement each other naturally.

"The best pairings create a harmony where both the cheese and wine taste better together than they do separately."

Red Wine Pairings

Red wines offer a wonderful range of pairing possibilities with cheese. Here are some of our favorite combinations:

Aged Cheddar with Cabernet Sauvignon

The bold tannins of Cabernet Sauvignon stand up beautifully to the sharp, crystalline texture of well-aged cheddar. Look for cheddars aged 2+ years for the best pairing.

Pro Tip: Add some dried fruit or nuts to your cheese board to bridge the flavors even further.

Gruyère with Pinot Noir

The earthy, nutty flavors of Gruyère find perfect balance with the fruity, medium-bodied characteristics of Pinot Noir. This Swiss classic is one of our most requested cheeses.

Creating Your Perfect Cheese Board

Now that you understand the pairings, here's how to assemble a show-stopping cheese board:

  1. Select 3-5 cheeses representing different styles (soft, hard, aged, blue)
  2. Choose complementary wines based on our pairing suggestions above
  3. Add accompaniments like fresh fruit, nuts, honey, and artisanal crackers
  4. Arrange thoughtfully with separate knives for each cheese and labels if serving to guests
  5. Serve at proper temperatures - cheese at room temperature, whites chilled, reds slightly cool

For quick reference, here's our cheat sheet for perfect pairings:
