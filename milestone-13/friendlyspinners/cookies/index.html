1. Understanding Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide valuable information to the website owners about user behavior and preferences. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your device.

In addition to traditional cookies, we may use other tracking technologies including:

  • Web beacons: Tiny graphics with unique identifiers used to track user movements
  • Local storage: Data stored in your browser for persistent information
  • Session storage: Temporary data storage during your browsing session
  • Fingerprinting: Techniques to identify devices based on configuration

2. Comprehensive Overview of Our Cookie Usage

We use cookies and similar technologies for several essential purposes that enhance your experience on our platform:

Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. Without these cookies, our website cannot operate effectively.

Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This data helps us improve how our website works and identify areas for enhancement.

Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced features and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we've added to our pages to remember your preferences and provide customized experiences.

Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies track browsing habits and are used to deliver targeted (interest-based) advertising. They remember that you've visited our website and may share this information with other organizations such as advertisers.
