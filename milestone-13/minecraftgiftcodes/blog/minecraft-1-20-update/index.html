Minecraft 1.20 Update: All New Features Explained

June 15, 2025 â€¢ 8 min read

The Minecraft 1.20 update, officially named the "Trails & Tales" update, has finally arrived, bringing a wealth of new features that expand the creative possibilities in Minecraft. This update focuses on self-expression through storytelling, adventure, and creativity, introducing elements that will change how you explore and interact with the world.

Cherry Blossom Biome

One of the most visually stunning additions is the new Cherry Blossom biome. These picturesque pink forests generate in mountainous areas and introduce several new elements:

  • Cherry Trees: With their distinctive pink leaves, these trees drop pink petals that create a beautiful falling effect
  • Cherry Wood Set: A complete new wood type including planks, logs, stripped logs, doors, trapdoors, and more
  • Pink Petals: A new ground cover block that can be placed in various configurations
  • Biome-Specific Mobs: Bees spawn more frequently in these biomes, making them ideal for honey farms

The Cherry Blossom biome isn't just beautiful - it's also functional. The wood type has a unique pink hue that opens up new building possibilities, and the biome's generation in mountains makes it easier to find than some other rare biomes.

Archaeology System

Minecraft 1.20 introduces a brand new gameplay system: Archaeology. This feature adds a new dimension to exploration and rewards careful examination of your surroundings.

Key Archaeology Components:

Item Function How to Obtain
Brush Used to excavate suspicious blocks Crafted with sticks, copper, and feathers
Suspicious Sand Contains hidden artifacts Found in desert temples and wells
Suspicious Gravel Contains hidden artifacts Found in ocean ruins
Pottery Shards Used to craft decorated pots Found in suspicious blocks
Sniffer Egg Hatches the ancient Sniffer mob Rare find in suspicious blocks

To use the archaeology system, you'll need to craft a brush (made with sticks, copper ingots, and feathers). Then look for suspicious sand in desert temples or suspicious gravel in ocean ruins. Carefully brush these blocks to reveal hidden treasures without destroying them.

Armor Trims and Customization

The 1.20 update introduces armor trims, allowing for unprecedented customization of your armor appearance. This system works similarly to banner patterns but for your armor.

How Armor Trims Work:

  1. Find armor trim smithing templates in various structures (each structure has unique designs)
  2. Combine the template with the appropriate material (copper, iron, gold, etc.) in a smithing table
  3. Apply the trim to any piece of armor
  4. Mix and match different patterns and materials for unique combinations
"The armor trim system adds a whole new layer of personalization to Minecraft. Now your diamond armor can look completely different from other players', reflecting your unique exploration history and style."

There are currently 16 different armor trim patterns to discover, each found in specific structures like ancient cities, desert temples, or jungle temples. The material you use for the trim (like gold or diamond) affects the color of the trim on your armor.

New Mob: The Sniffer

After winning the Minecraft Live 2022 mob vote, the Sniffer has finally made its debut in the 1.20 update. This ancient mob brings several unique mechanics:

  • Passive Behavior: Sniffers are completely passive and won't attack players or other mobs
  • Egg Hatching: You can only obtain Sniffers by hatching Sniffer eggs found through archaeology
  • Plant Discovery: Adult Sniffers will occasionally sniff out ancient seeds that grow into new decorative plants
  • Size: One of the largest passive mobs in the game, similar in size to a ravager

The plants that Sniffers discover are purely decorative but include several new flora options that can't be obtained any other way. This makes Sniffers valuable for builders looking to add unique vegetation to their creations.

New Blocks and Items

Beyond the major features, Minecraft 1.20 adds numerous smaller but equally exciting blocks and items:

Notable Additions:

  • Bamboo Wood Set: Complete wood type including doors, trapdoors, and a unique bamboo mosaic block
  • Hanging Signs: Vertical signs that can be hung from chains or attached to the underside of blocks
  • Chiseled Bookshelf: Functional bookshelf that shows which slots contain books and allows specific retrieval
  • Camels: New rideable mob found in desert villages with a unique sitting/dashing mechanic
  • Piglin Head: First mob head that can be obtained in survival without commands

The bamboo wood set is particularly noteworthy as it provides a fast-growing wood alternative that looks distinct from existing wood types. The chiseled bookshelf solves the long-standing issue of not knowing which books are where in your bookshelves.

Technical Improvements

Beyond the visible additions, Minecraft 1.20 includes several under-the-hood improvements:

  • Performance Optimizations: Better rendering performance, especially for large builds
  • Inventory Management: Improved creative inventory with better categorization
  • Accessibility: New font scaling options and improved text-to-speech functionality
  • Multiplayer: Reduced latency in certain network conditions

Conclusion

The Minecraft 1.20 Trails & Tales update represents a significant expansion of the game's creative and exploration possibilities. With archaeology adding a new dimension to world exploration, armor trims enabling unprecedented customization, and the beautiful Cherry Blossom biomes offering new building materials, this update has something for every type of Minecraft player.

Whether you're a builder looking for new aesthetic options, an explorer seeking fresh challenges, or a redstone engineer excited by the new functional blocks, 1.20 delivers meaningful content that will change how you play. The update's focus on self-expression through the items and blocks you discover and create truly embodies Minecraft's core philosophy of creativity and storytelling.
