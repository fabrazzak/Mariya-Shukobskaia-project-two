Our Commitment

At Rollic Games, we believe gaming should be fun, safe, and enjoyable for everyone. We're committed to promoting responsible gaming practices and providing tools to help players maintain healthy gaming habits.

While our games are designed for entertainment, we recognize that some players may need help managing their gameplay. This page provides information about our responsible gaming policies, tools, and resources available to players and parents.

We continuously work to improve our responsible gaming measures and collaborate with industry organizations to maintain best practices in player protection.

Our Responsible Gaming Policies

Measures we've implemented to promote healthy gaming habits

Age-Appropriate Content

All our games are clearly rated according to international standards. We provide detailed content descriptors to help players and parents make informed choices.

We support device-level parental controls and provide guidance to parents on managing in-app purchases, play time, and content access for younger players.

Player Support

Player Resources

Tools and information to support healthy gaming habits

Self-Assessment Tool

Ask yourself these questions to evaluate your gaming habits:

  • Do I play for longer than I intended to?
  • Does gaming interfere with my daily responsibilities?
  • Have I tried to cut back on gaming without success?
  • Do I feel irritable when I can't play?
  • Has gaming caused problems in my relationships?

If you answered "yes" to several of these questions, you may want to evaluate your gaming habits or seek support.

Tips for Healthy Gaming

Set Time Limits

Decide in advance how long you'll play and set alarms to remind you when to stop.

Track Spending

Keep a record of in-game purchases and set monthly budgets for gaming expenses.

Take Regular Breaks

Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

Balance Activities

Make time for other hobbies, social activities, and physical exercise.

For Parents

Guidance on managing your child's gaming experience

Parental Controls Guide

We recommend using device-level parental controls to manage your child's gaming experience. Here's how to set them up:

Android Devices

Use Google's Family Link app to manage apps, set time limits, and control purchases.

iOS Devices

Use Screen Time in Settings to set app limits, content restrictions, and require approval for purchases.

General Tips

  • Play games with your child to understand the content
  • Set clear rules about when and how long they can play
  • Keep gaming devices in common areas, not bedrooms
  • Encourage a balance of activities beyond screens

Conversation Starters

Talking openly with your child about gaming is important. Try these questions:

"What do you enjoy most about this game?"

"How do you feel when you've been playing for a long time?"

"What other activities do you enjoy besides gaming?"

"How can we make sure gaming doesn't interfere with other important things?"
