Nintendo Direct June 2025 Highlights: All the Big Announcements

The June 2025 Nintendo Direct has just concluded, delivering a packed 40-minute presentation filled with exciting announcements, game reveals, and updates for the Nintendo Switch platform. Here's everything you need to know from today's showcase.

Direct Overview and Key Announcements

Nintendo kicked off their summer presentation with a bang, revealing several major first-party titles alongside surprising third-party collaborations. The Direct focused heavily on games coming in the second half of 2025, with a few surprises for early 2026.

Key statistics from the presentation:

  • 15 new game announcements
  • 8 release date reveals
  • 3 major DLC expansions
  • 2 surprise shadow drops (games available immediately after the Direct)
  • 1 new hardware announcement

Pro Tip for Nintendo Fans

Many of the announced games are available for pre-order now on the Nintendo eShop. Pre-ordering often gives you bonus content and ensures you can play the game the minute it's released.

Major Game Announcements

1. Super Mario Odyssey 2 (November 21, 2025)

The sequel to the beloved 2017 platformer takes Mario to entirely new kingdoms with fresh capture mechanics. The trailer showed Mario possessing new objects and enemies, including a massive dragon that can fly between floating islands.

2. The Legend of Zelda: Echoes of the Past (September 12, 2025)

This new 2D Zelda adventure features a time-shifting mechanic where players can switch between past and present versions of the world to solve puzzles and overcome obstacles. Developed by the team behind A Link Between Worlds.

3. Metroid Prime 4: Darkness (October 31, 2025)

After years of development, Retro Studios finally showed gameplay of the long-awaited Metroid Prime sequel. The footage revealed new beam weapons, terrifying alien creatures, and massive environments to explore.

4. Pokémon Legends: Celebi (Early 2026)

Following the success of Pokémon Legends: Arceus, Game Freak is taking players to the Johto region in this historical adventure featuring the time-traveling Mythical Pokémon Celebi.

5. Super Smash Bros. Ultimate - New Challengers Pack (Available Now)

In a surprise announcement, Nintendo revealed five new fighters coming to Smash Ultimate: Crash Bandicoot, Master Chief, Dante (Devil May Cry), 2B (NieR: Automata), and Isaac (Golden Sun).

6. Splatoon 4 (March 2026)

The ink-based shooter returns with new weapons, movement options, and a completely overhauled single-player campaign that takes the Inklings and Octolings into space.

"This might be Nintendo's strongest Direct in years. The combination of fan-requested sequels and bold new ideas shows Nintendo at the top of their game." - Sarah Miller, Gaming Journalist

Shadow Drops (Available Immediately)

Nintendo surprised viewers with two games available right after the Direct:

1. F-Zero 99 (Free for Nintendo Switch Online members)

Following the success of Tetris 99 and Pac-Man 99, Nintendo applies the battle royale formula to F-Zero, with 99 players racing simultaneously on classic tracks.

2. Golden Sun Remastered ($29.99)

A complete visual overhaul of the beloved GBA RPG, featuring quality-of-life improvements and a new epilogue chapter.

Third-Party Highlights

Nintendo showcased several notable third-party titles coming to Switch:

  • Persona 6 - Coming to Switch day-and-date with other platforms (February 14, 2026)
  • Dragon Quest XII: The Flames of Fate - Exclusive gameplay showing the Switch version (Summer 2026)
  • Hollow Knight: Silksong - Finally confirmed for September 25, 2025
  • Octopath Traveler II: Director's Cut - Enhanced version with new content (November 2025)

DLC and Updates

Several major Nintendo games are receiving substantial updates:

1. Mario Kart 8 Deluxe - Booster Course Pass Wave 6 (July 15, 2025)

The final wave includes 8 new tracks, including fan-favorites from Mario Kart Tour and two brand-new original courses.

2. Animal Crossing: New Horizons - Final Major Update (June 30, 2025)

Nintendo is sending off their life sim with one last free update adding farming, new villagers, and quality-of-life improvements.

3. Xenoblade Chronicles 3: Future Redeemed Part 2 (August 2025)

This story expansion continues where the first DLC left off, promising to conclude the trilogy's narrative.

Hardware Announcement

While rumors of a "Switch Pro" were rampant, Nintendo instead announced:

Nintendo Switch Lite - OLED Model (July 25, 2025)

A new version of the Switch Lite featuring an OLED screen, improved battery life, and new color options (including transparent purple, a callback to the Game Boy Color).

Final Thoughts

The June 2025 Nintendo Direct delivered an impressive lineup of games that should keep Switch owners busy well into 2026. With a mix of long-awaited sequels, surprising revivals, and exciting new hardware, Nintendo continues to show why the Switch remains one of the most compelling gaming platforms.
