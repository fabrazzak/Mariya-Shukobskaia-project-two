Our Coffee Story

From bad office coffee to specialty roasting - how Sumato Coffee became the fuel for America's workplaces

Meet Ben Martinez

The health-focused executive who carried coffee books in his backpack and filmed his pre-dawn brewing routine. After 20 years of corporate life across six states and six companies, Ben turned his coffee obsession into a mission: bringing specialty coffee culture to the workplace.

In 2017, what began as a passion project quickly grew into Sumato Coffee Company. Ben's vision was simple but revolutionary - workplace coffee shouldn't be an afterthought. It should be an experience worth looking forward to, a moment of quality in the daily grind.

Today, Ben leads a team of specialty coffee roasters and bean geeks from our headquarters in the mountains of Utah, still cupping every batch and testing every brew method to ensure Sumato meets his exacting standards.

Our Journey

From a single frustrated coffee lover to a company serving workplaces across America

2015 - The Breaking Point

After choking down yet another pot of stale, bitter office coffee in Salt Lake City, Ben Martinez decides enough is enough. He begins researching specialty coffee and experimenting with home roasting in his garage.

2016 - Coffee Underground

Ben starts bringing his home-roasted coffee to work, sharing it with colleagues. Word spreads through the office, then to neighboring businesses. What begins as a passion project gains unexpected demand.

2017 - Official Launch

Sumato Coffee Company is officially founded with a small 5kg roaster and a simple website. The first blends - "The Sender" and "Rise & Grinder" - become instant favorites among Utah offices.

2018 - First Warehouse

With demand growing, Sumato moves into a dedicated roasting facility in Park City, Utah. The team grows to include two full-time roasters and a customer service manager.

2019 - National Expansion

Sumato begins shipping nationwide, with particular popularity in tech hubs like San Francisco, Austin, and New York. The subscription service launches, solving the "we ran out of coffee" office emergency.

2020 - Pandemic Pivot

As offices empty due to COVID-19, Sumato quickly adapts by offering home subscriptions and virtual coffee tastings. Surprisingly, business grows as remote workers seek better home coffee setups.

2021 - New Roastery

Sumato upgrades to a 25kg Diedrich roaster and expands the team to 12 employees. The company begins direct trade relationships with farms in Ethiopia and Guatemala.

2023 - Retail Launch

After years of focusing on workplaces, Sumato opens its first retail location in Salt Lake City, featuring a tasting room and brewing classes. The "Work From Home" blend becomes a bestseller.

Today - Growing Strong

Sumato now serves over 1,200 businesses and 15,000 home subscribers across North America. Despite growth, we still roast in small batches, cup every lot, and maintain our founding belief: great coffee makes work better.
