2025 Home Decor Trends: What's In and What's Out

From bold colors to sustainable materials, discover the trends shaping interior design in 2025

As we move into 2025, the world of interior design is embracing bold new directions while saying goodbye to some tired trends. Whether you're planning a complete home makeover or just looking to refresh your space, understanding these emerging trends can help you create a home that feels both current and timeless. Here's our comprehensive guide to what's in and what's out for 2025 home decor.

What's In for 2025

1. Bold, Saturated Colors

After years of neutral dominance, 2025 is all about embracing color with confidence. Deep emerald greens, rich sapphire blues, and vibrant terracotta oranges are leading the charge. These saturated hues work particularly well when used on walls, cabinetry, or large furniture pieces. The key is to balance these bold colors with neutral elements to prevent overwhelming a space.

2. Sustainable and Natural Materials

Eco-conscious design continues to gain momentum with these trending materials:

  • Reclaimed wood with visible imperfections
  • Recycled glass countertops and tiles
  • Organic linen and hemp textiles
  • Cork flooring and wall coverings
  • Plant-based leather alternatives

These materials aren't just environmentally friendly—they add warmth and texture that synthetic alternatives can't match.

3. Curved Furniture and Architectural Elements

The sharp edges of modern design are giving way to softer, more organic shapes. Look for:

  • Rounded sofas and sectionals
  • Oval and circular dining tables
  • Arched doorways and built-in niches
  • Curved headboards and banquettes

These flowing forms create a more welcoming and comfortable atmosphere in any room.

4. Mixed Metals and Finishes

The days of matching all your hardware are over. 2025 celebrates the artful combination of different metal finishes. Try pairing:

  • Brushed brass with matte black
  • Polished nickel with aged bronze
  • Stainless steel with copper accents

The key is to limit yourself to 2-3 finishes per room and repeat them consistently throughout the space.

5. Maximalist Art Displays

Gallery walls are evolving from carefully curated collections to exuberant, layered displays. Popular approaches include:

  • Mixing frame styles and sizes
  • Incorporating three-dimensional objects
  • Combining original art with affordable prints
  • Extending displays from walls onto ceilings

What's Out for 2025

1. All-White Everything

While white kitchens and bathrooms aren't disappearing entirely, the stark all-white aesthetic is losing ground to warmer, more personality-filled spaces. Instead of pure white, consider:

  • Creamy off-whites
  • Very light greiges
  • Pale, warm grays

2. Matchy-Matchy Furniture Sets

Pre-coordinated living room or bedroom sets are being replaced by thoughtfully mixed pieces. The new approach creates more interesting, collected-over-time looks with:

  • Different but complementary wood tones
  • Varied leg styles on seating and tables
  • Combined vintage and contemporary pieces

3. Open Shelving in Kitchens

While open shelving had its moment, 2025 sees a return to closed storage solutions that:

  • Keep dust off dishes
  • Allow for less-perfect organization
  • Create cleaner visual lines

Glass-front cabinets offer a happy medium for those who want some display space.

4. Overly Industrial Styles

The raw, unfinished industrial look is softening with:

  • Warmer metal finishes replacing cold steel
  • More wood and fabric elements
  • Softer lighting options

5. Word Art and Clichéd Signs

"Live Laugh Love" and similar generic word art are making way for:

  • Original artwork
  • Personalized family name signs
  • Vintage finds with character

How to Incorporate 2025 Trends Thoughtfully

While it's fun to experiment with trends, the most successful interiors balance current styles with timeless elements. Here's how to approach 2025 trends:

  • Use bold colors in easily changeable elements like throw pillows or accent walls
  • Invest in quality, sustainable pieces for big-ticket items
  • Mix a few trendy pieces with your existing decor
  • Stay true to your personal style—not every trend will work for every home
"A room should never allow the eye to settle in one place. It should smile at you and create fantasy." - Juan Montoya

Remember, the best homes evolve gradually. You don't need to overhaul your space to make it feel fresh—sometimes just a few strategic updates can make all the difference. Which 2025 trends are you most excited to try in your home?
