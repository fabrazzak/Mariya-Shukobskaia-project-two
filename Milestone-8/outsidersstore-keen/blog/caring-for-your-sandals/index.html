Caring for Your Sandals

A Seasonal Guide to Maintenance and Storage

April 28, 2025 â€¢ 6 min read

Quality sandals are an investment in comfort and style, but without proper care, even the best sandals can deteriorate quickly. This seasonal guide will walk you through maintaining your sandals throughout the year, from daily cleaning to off-season storage, ensuring they stay comfortable and last for many seasons.

Spring: Post-Winter Revival

After months in storage, your sandals need some attention before their first spring outing:

1. Inspection

Examine your sandals for any signs of damage or dry rot. Check straps, buckles, and footbeds for wear. Look for cracking in leather or dried-out adhesives.

2. Deep Cleaning

Remove dust and storage debris with these steps:

  • Brush away loose dirt with a soft brush
  • Use a mild soap solution (1 tsp dish soap per cup of water)
  • Scrub gently with a soft-bristle brush
  • Rinse thoroughly with clean water
  • Air dry completely away from direct heat

Pro Tip:

For leather sandals, apply a conditioner after cleaning to restore moisture and flexibility to the material.

Summer: Regular Maintenance

Summer is when your sandals get the most use and need regular care:

1. After-Use Cleaning

For frequent use, establish a quick cleaning routine:

  • Wipe down with a damp cloth after each wear
  • Remove insoles periodically to clean underneath
  • Use a toothbrush for cleaning between straps

2. Odor Control

Combat summer sweat and odors with these methods:

  • Sprinkle baking soda inside and let sit overnight
  • Use a 50/50 water-vinegar solution for wiping
  • Consider antimicrobial sprays for persistent odors

3. Saltwater Exposure

If you wear sandals to the beach:

  • Rinse immediately after saltwater exposure
  • Pay special attention to metal hardware
  • Dry thoroughly before storing

Fall: Preparation for Storage

As temperatures drop, prepare your sandals for winter storage:

1. End-of-Season Cleaning

Give your sandals a thorough cleaning before storing:

  • Remove all dirt and debris
  • Treat any stains with appropriate cleaners
  • Condition leather components
  • Ensure complete dryness before storage

2. Hardware Check

Inspect and maintain all metal components:

  • Clean buckles and rivets with a soft cloth
  • Apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly to prevent corrosion
  • Tighten any loose screws or fasteners

Storage Tip:

Stuff the footbed with acid-free tissue paper to help maintain shape during storage.

Winter: Proper Storage

How you store sandals during winter affects their longevity:

1. Storage Environment

Choose the right location:

  • Cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
  • Avoid damp basements or hot attics
  • Consider a climate-controlled space if possible

2. Storage Methods

Proper storage techniques:

  • Store in breathable cotton bags (not plastic)
  • Keep in their original shape (don't fold or bend)
  • Separate pairs to prevent color transfer

3. Periodic Checks

Even in storage, check periodically for:

  • Moisture buildup
  • Pest activity
  • Material degradation

Year-Round Care Tips

These maintenance practices apply regardless of season:

1. Rotation

Rotate between multiple pairs to extend the life of each.

2. Professional Help

For high-end sandals, consider professional cleaning and repair services annually.

3. Replacement Schedule

Even with perfect care, sandals typically last:

  • 3-5 years for daily use pairs
  • 5-7 years for occasional wear

With this seasonal care routine, your favorite sandals will provide comfort and style for years to come. Remember that prevention is easier than restoration - regular maintenance beats intensive repairs every time.
