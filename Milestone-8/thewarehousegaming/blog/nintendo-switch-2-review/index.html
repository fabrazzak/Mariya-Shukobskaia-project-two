REVIEW NINTENDO CONSOLE

Nintendo Switch 2 Review: The Next Level of Portable Gaming

June 15, 2025 • 8 min read • 4.5/5

After weeks of hands-on experience with Nintendo's highly anticipated Switch 2, we can confidently say this console represents a significant leap forward in portable gaming technology. Building on the massive success of the original Switch, this new iteration delivers improved performance, better battery life, and innovative features that set it apart from both its predecessor and current competitors in the handheld market.

Design and Build Quality

At first glance, the Switch 2 maintains the familiar hybrid design that made its predecessor so popular, but with several refinements. The console is slightly larger (8.5 inches compared to the original's 6.2 inches), with a 7-inch OLED display that boasts a 1080p resolution in handheld mode - a welcome upgrade from the 720p screen on the original Switch.

The Joy-Con controllers have been redesigned with improved ergonomics, addressing one of the main complaints about the original model. They're slightly larger and feature more pronounced grips, making them more comfortable for extended play sessions. The analog sticks feel more robust and precise, hopefully putting an end to the dreaded "Joy-Con drift" issue that plagued earlier models.

Technical Specifications

Under the hood, the Switch 2 represents a substantial upgrade over its predecessor. Nintendo has partnered with Nvidia to develop a custom Tegra processor that delivers significantly better performance while maintaining excellent battery efficiency.

Performance and Gameplay

The performance improvements are immediately noticeable when playing games. Load times are significantly reduced, and frame rates are much more stable, even in demanding titles. We tested several games, including a new Zelda title developed specifically for the Switch 2, and were impressed by the smooth performance and improved visual fidelity.

In handheld mode, the 1080p OLED display is gorgeous, with vibrant colors and deep blacks. The increased screen size makes a noticeable difference in immersion, especially for games with detailed environments. When docked, the console supports 4K upscaling, which works surprisingly well to enhance image quality on large displays.

New Features

Nintendo has introduced several innovative features with the Switch 2:

  • Adaptive Refresh Rate: The display can dynamically adjust its refresh rate between 60Hz and 120Hz depending on the content, improving both battery life and smoothness.
  • Improved Haptic Feedback: The new Joy-Cons feature more precise haptics, allowing for more nuanced feedback in games.
  • Backward Compatibility: The Switch 2 plays all original Switch games, with many receiving automatic performance enhancements.
  • Enhanced Audio: The speakers have been upgraded to provide clearer, more immersive sound.
  • Bluetooth Audio: Finally, native Bluetooth headphone support is included.

Battery Life

Battery life was one of our primary concerns going into this review, given the more powerful hardware. However, Nintendo has managed to maintain respectable battery performance. In our testing with various games at medium brightness:

  • Demanding 3D games: ~5 hours
  • Less demanding/2D games: ~7-8 hours
  • Video playback: ~9 hours

While not quite matching the battery life of some dedicated handhelds, this represents an improvement over the original Switch, especially considering the more powerful hardware. The console also supports fast charging, reaching 50% in about 30 minutes.

Pros

  • Significant performance upgrade over original Switch
  • Beautiful 1080p OLED display
  • Improved Joy-Con design and ergonomics
  • Good battery life for the power
  • Full backward compatibility
  • 4K upscaling in docked mode

Cons

  • More expensive than original Switch at launch
  • Still not as powerful as some competitors
  • No expandable storage in base model
  • Some accessories not compatible with original Switch

Game Library

At launch, the Switch 2 has a strong lineup of games, including enhanced versions of popular Switch titles and several new exclusives. Nintendo has confirmed that all future first-party titles will be developed with the Switch 2 as the primary platform, though many will still release on the original Switch.

"The Switch 2 represents Nintendo's most significant hardware leap in a decade, offering a perfect balance of portability and performance that stays true to the company's philosophy of accessible, innovative gaming."

Some standout launch titles include:

  • The Legend of Zelda: Echoes of the Past - A stunning new Zelda adventure built specifically for the Switch 2
  • Mario Kart 9 - The latest installment with improved graphics and new track mechanics
  • Metroid Prime 4 - Finally released with visuals that rival current-gen consoles
  • Enhanced versions of popular Switch games like Breath of the Wild and Mario Odyssey with improved performance and visuals

Final Verdict

The Nintendo Switch 2 is a remarkable evolution of the hybrid console concept that made its predecessor so successful. It addresses nearly all the shortcomings of the original model while introducing meaningful improvements that enhance the gaming experience.

While it may not match the raw power of some competitors, the Switch 2 offers the best combination of portability, performance, and game library in the current market. The improved display, better battery life, and enhanced controllers make it a joy to use in both handheld and docked modes.

For existing Switch owners, the upgrade may not be essential unless you're craving better performance and visuals. However, for new buyers or those who spend significant time playing in handheld mode, the Switch 2 is easily the best portable gaming system Nintendo has ever made.

Nintendo Switch 2 Rating

4.5/5

Bottom Line: The Nintendo Switch 2 is a substantial upgrade that delivers on its promises while staying true to Nintendo's philosophy of accessible, innovative gaming. It's the best hybrid console on the market and comes highly recommended for both new and existing Nintendo fans.
