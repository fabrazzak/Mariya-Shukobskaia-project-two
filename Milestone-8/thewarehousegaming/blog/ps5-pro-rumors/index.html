PS5 Pro Review: Is It Worth the Upgrade?

July 2, 2025 • 10 min read • 4/5

Sony's PS5 Pro represents the company's latest effort to push console gaming performance to new heights. After spending two weeks with this premium console, we've put it through its paces to determine whether it's worth the premium price tag over the standard PS5, especially for those who already own Sony's current-gen system.

Design and Build

The PS5 Pro maintains the same distinctive design language as the original PS5 but with some subtle refinements. The console is slightly more compact (about 10% smaller) and features a matte black finish with blue LED accents that give it a more premium, understated look compared to the standard model's white panels.

The biggest physical change is the new modular design that allows for easier access to internal components. The faceplates are now tool-less and can be removed with a simple button press, revealing the SSD expansion slot and dust catchers. This makes maintenance and upgrades significantly easier than with the original PS5.

Technical Specifications

Where the PS5 Pro really shines is under the hood. Sony has packed this console with cutting-edge hardware that delivers a substantial performance boost over the standard PS5:

Specification PS5 Pro Standard PS5
CPU Zen 4, 8-core @ 4.4GHz Zen 2, 8-core @ 3.5GHz
GPU RDNA 3, 36 CUs @ 2.8GHz RDNA 2, 36 CUs @ 2.23GHz
Memory 16GB GDDR6 @ 18Gbps 16GB GDDR6 @ 14Gbps
Storage 1TB PCIe 5.0 SSD 825GB PCIe 4.0 SSD
Throughput 12.8GB/s (compressed) 8-9GB/s (compressed)
Ray Tracing Dedicated hardware Software-based
Max Resolution 8K @ 60Hz / 4K @ 120Hz 8K @ 30Hz / 4K @ 120Hz

Performance and Gaming Experience

In real-world testing, the PS5 Pro delivers noticeably better performance across all types of games. Here's what we observed in our benchmark tests:

Native 4K Gaming

The PS5 Pro can maintain native 4K resolution at stable frame rates in most games, something the standard PS5 often struggles with (typically using dynamic resolution scaling). In titles like God of War: Ragnarök and Horizon Forbidden West, the difference in image clarity is immediately apparent.

120Hz Gameplay

More games can now take advantage of 120Hz refresh rates at higher resolutions. We tested several competitive shooters and racing games at 1440p/120Hz, and the experience was buttery smooth with virtually no screen tearing thanks to the improved VRR support.

Ray Tracing Performance

The dedicated ray tracing hardware makes the biggest difference. Games with intensive lighting effects like Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart and Spider-Man 2 run with full ray tracing enabled at higher frame rates, whereas the standard PS5 often has to make compromises.

Pros

  • Significant performance boost over standard PS5
  • Native 4K gaming at stable frame rates
  • Excellent ray tracing performance
  • More compact and premium design
  • Faster load times with PCIe 5.0 SSD
  • Backward compatible with all PS5 games

Cons

  • High price point ($699)
  • Limited exclusive features at launch
  • Only marginal improvement for 1080p gamers
  • Same controller as standard PS5

Exclusive Features

The PS5 Pro introduces several features that aren't available on the standard model:

  • PlayStation Spectral Super Resolution (PSSR): Sony's answer to DLSS, this AI upscaling technology delivers near-native quality at higher frame rates.
  • Enhanced Game Boost: Automatically improves frame rates and resolutions for select PS4 and PS5 games.
  • DualSense Pro Support: New controller features like adaptive triggers with finer granularity (though the controller itself must be purchased separately).
  • 8K Media Playback: Support for 8K video streaming services (though native 8K gaming remains limited).

Heat and Noise Levels

Despite the increased performance, Sony has managed to keep the PS5 Pro relatively cool and quiet. The new vapor chamber cooling system is more efficient than the standard PS5's solution. During extended gaming sessions, we measured:

  • Noise levels: 32-38 dB (compared to 40-45 dB on standard PS5)
  • Exhaust temperature: 45-50°C (5-7°C cooler than standard PS5)

The console does get warm to the touch during intensive gaming, but never uncomfortably so. The improved thermal design should theoretically lead to better long-term reliability as well.

Is It Worth the Upgrade?

This is the million-dollar question for existing PS5 owners. Based on our testing, here's who should consider upgrading:

  • 4K TV owners: If you have a high-end 4K display (especially with 120Hz support), you'll see the most benefit from the PS5 Pro's improved performance.
  • Ray tracing enthusiasts: The dedicated hardware makes a noticeable difference in games that support advanced lighting effects.
  • Competitive gamers: The higher frame rates at 1440p will give you an edge in fast-paced games.

However, if you're gaming on a 1080p display or aren't particularly sensitive to frame rate and resolution differences, the standard PS5 remains a capable console that will continue to receive all the same games.

Final Verdict

The PS5 Pro is undoubtedly the most powerful PlayStation console ever made, offering tangible improvements over the standard PS5 in nearly every area. The enhanced performance, better cooling, and premium design make it an attractive option for serious gamers with high-end displays.

That said, the $699 price tag is steep, especially when you consider that you'll need a compatible display to fully appreciate the upgrades. For most casual gamers, the standard PS5 remains the better value proposition. But for enthusiasts who demand the absolute best console gaming experience, the PS5 Pro delivers in spades.

PS5 Pro Rating

4/5

Bottom Line: The PS5 Pro is a premium console for premium gamers. It offers meaningful improvements over the standard PS5, but the high price and requirement for compatible displays mean it's not for everyone. If you have the right setup and want the best possible PlayStation experience, it's worth the investment.
