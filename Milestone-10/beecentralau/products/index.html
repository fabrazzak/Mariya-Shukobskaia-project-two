FAQS

Frequently Asked Questions

Our honey is derived from the nectar of WA native Leptospermum trees, which contain naturally high levels of Methylglyoxal (MGO) and other compounds that give it exceptional antibacterial activity. Independent testing shows our honey has activity levels comparable to or exceeding New Zealand Manuka honey.

Store your honey in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. There's no need to refrigerate - in fact, cold temperatures can cause crystallization. If your honey does crystallize (a natural process), simply place the jar in warm water to return it to liquid form.

Our Leptospermum essential oil can be used in several ways: diluted with a carrier oil for skin application, added to a diffuser for aromatherapy, mixed into homemade skincare products, or used in a steam inhalation for respiratory benefits. Always do a patch test first and consult with a healthcare professional if you have sensitive skin or medical conditions.
