Project Update

The Bee Shed: Construction Milestones Reached in York

March 18, 2025 6 min read
Photo: Bee Central Construction Team

Bee Central is excited to announce that construction of The Bee Shed discovery centre in York has reached several key milestones, bringing us closer to our vision of creating a world-class educational and research facility dedicated to honey bees and active honey production.

Foundation Work Completed

The first major phase of construction, including all foundation work and underground utilities, was successfully completed in February. This critical stage involved:

  • Excavation and pouring of reinforced concrete foundations
  • Installation of underground plumbing and electrical conduits
  • Construction of the stormwater management system
  • Completion of the geothermal heating/cooling well system

"The foundation work went exceptionally smoothly thanks to the expertise of our construction team and favorable weather conditions," said project manager Sarah Wilkins. "We're particularly proud of implementing sustainable geothermal technology that will significantly reduce the building's energy consumption."

By the Numbers

  • Concrete poured: 285 cubic meters
  • Underground piping: 1.2 kilometers
  • Geothermal wells: 18 (120m depth)
  • Work hours: 3,450 hours

Sustainable Features

  • Geothermal heating/cooling system
  • Solar-ready roof design
  • Rainwater harvesting
  • Native landscaping
  • Bee-friendly green roof sections

Structural Framework Progress

With foundations complete, the project has now moved into the structural framing phase. As of this update:

  • Steel framework for the main exhibition hall is 75% complete
  • Timber framing for the educational wing is fully erected
  • Roof trusses for the retail and cafe areas are installed
  • Structural work for the auditorium is underway

The distinctive architectural feature of the building - a hexagonal "honeycomb" skylight structure - is currently being fabricated off-site and will be installed in the coming weeks. This centerpiece will flood the main exhibition space with natural light while symbolizing the building's purpose.

Research Facilities Taking Shape

The University of Western Australia's research wing is progressing rapidly, with specialized features including:

Facility Description Status
Honey Analysis Lab State-of-the-art facility for testing active honey properties Walls up
Bee Observation Area Live bee colonies with research access points Framing
Microscopy Suite Advanced imaging for bee health research Walls up
Tissue Culture Lab For studying bee pathogens and treatments Foundation
