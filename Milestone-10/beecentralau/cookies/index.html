Cookies Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Bee Central's Cookies Policy. This policy explains how Bee Central ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website at beecentralau.com (the "Site"). By using our Site, you consent to the use of cookies and similar technologies as described in this policy, unless you have disabled them in your browser settings or through our cookie consent tool. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which provides more detailed information about how we collect, use, and protect your personal data.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. Cookies typically contain the name of the website they come from, their "lifetime" (how long they stay on your device), and a value, which is usually a randomly generated unique number. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. They are uniquely assigned to you and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of our Site. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. You may disable these by changing your browser settings, but this may affect how the website functions. Essential cookies on our Site include:

  • Session cookies: These temporary cookies remain in your browser's cookie file until you leave the Site. They allow us to link your actions during a browser session.
  • Authentication cookies: Used to identify you when you log into secure areas of our Site.
  • Security cookies: Used to detect malicious activities and protect user data.

3.2 Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our Site, such as which pages are visited most often and if users receive error messages. These cookies don't collect information that identifies individual visitors. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. It is only used to improve how our Site works. Examples include:

  • Google Analytics cookies: These help us understand how users interact with our Site by collecting and reporting information anonymously.
  • Heatmap cookies: These show us where users click and scroll on our pages to help us improve layout and content placement.

3.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow our Site to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. They may also be used to provide services you have requested, such as watching a video or commenting on a blog. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites. Examples include:

  • Language preference cookies: Remember your language selection for future visits.
  • Video player cookies: Remember your playback preferences for embedded videos.
  • Form completion cookies: Remember information you've entered in forms to save you time on subsequent visits.

3.4 Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They remember that you have visited our Site and this information may be shared with other organizations such as advertisers. Examples include:

  • Social media cookies: Used when you share information using a social media sharing button or "like" button on our Site.
  • Retargeting cookies: Allow our advertising partners to show you ads on other sites based on your browsing behavior on our Site.
  • Affiliate cookies: Identify that you came to our Site through an affiliate link so we can properly credit our partners.

4. Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar tracking technologies on our Site:

  • Web beacons: Small graphic images (also known as "pixel tags" or "clear GIFs") that may be included on our Site and in our emails to track user activity.
  • Local Storage Objects (LSOs): Such as HTML5 local storage used to store content information and preferences.
  • ETags: A mechanism used by web servers to determine whether a component in the browser cache matches the original on the server.
  • Fingerprinting: Collecting and combining various pieces of information from your browser to create a unique "fingerprint" of your device.

5. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for several purposes, including:

  • Authentication: To recognize you when you visit our Site and to authenticate you when you log into secure areas.
  • Preferences: To remember information about you such as your preferred language and other settings.
  • Security: To enable and support our security features, and to help us detect malicious activity.
  • Performance: To monitor and improve the performance of our Site and services.
  • Analytics: To understand how our Site is used, track Site performance, and make improvements.
  • Advertising: To deliver targeted advertising based on your browsing activities and interests.
  • Social Media: To enable you to share content from our Site on social media platforms.
  • E-commerce: To maintain your shopping cart and process transactions securely.

6. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics of our Site, deliver advertisements on and through our Site, and so on. Some of these third-party services may be located in countries outside of Australia. These third-party providers are responsible for the cookies they set on our Site. We have no control over these cookies. Some specific third-party cookies we use include:

  • Google Analytics: For analyzing Site traffic and user behavior.
  • Facebook Pixel: For measuring the effectiveness of our Facebook ads and building audiences for future ads.
  • Hotjar: For understanding user behavior through heatmaps and session recordings.
  • YouTube: For embedding videos on our Site.
  • Stripe: For processing payments on our e-commerce platform.

7. Cookie Duration

The length of time a cookie stays on your device depends on whether it is a "persistent" or "session" cookie. Session cookies are temporary and remain only until you close your browser. Persistent cookies stay on your device until they expire or are deleted. The specific durations for cookies we use vary:

  • Session cookies: Expire when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: Typically last between 30 days to 2 years, depending on their purpose.
  • Authentication cookies: May last for the duration of your session or up to 30 days if you select "Remember Me".
  • Analytics cookies: Generally persist for 2 years unless you clear them.
  • Advertising cookies: Typically last for 30 to 90 days but may be refreshed if you revisit our Site.

8. Managing Cookies

8.1 Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow some control of most cookies through the browser settings. You can set your browser to accept or reject all, or certain, cookies. You can also set your browser to prompt you each time a cookie is offered. Below are links to instructions for managing cookies in common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Internet Explorer

Please note that if you disable cookies entirely, our Site may not function properly. Certain features of our Site may not be available, and some pages may not display correctly.

8.2 Our Cookie Consent Tool

When you first visit our Site, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customize your cookie preferences. You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of our Site. The tool will remember your preferences and apply them to future visits, unless you clear your browser cookies or use a different device or browser.

9. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that, when enabled, sends a signal to websites to request that your browsing is not tracked. Currently, our Site does not respond to DNT signals because no common, industry-accepted standard for DNT has been adopted by regulators, including no consistent standard of interpreting a DNT signal. We will continue to monitor developments around DNT browser technology and the implementation of a standard.

Your continued use of our Site after any changes to this policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
