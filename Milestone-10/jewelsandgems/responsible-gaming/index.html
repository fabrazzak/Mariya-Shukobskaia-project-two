Warning Signs

Recognizing when gaming might be becoming problematic

Time Management

Spending increasing amounts of time gaming at the expense of other activities.

Emotional Impact

Feeling irritable, anxious, or angry when unable to play or when losing.

Social Withdrawal

Losing interest in social activities or relationships due to gaming.

Financial Issues

Spending more on in-game purchases than intended or can afford.

Sleep Disruption

Staying up late or losing sleep to continue playing games.

Neglecting Responsibilities

Failing to meet work, school, or family obligations due to gaming.

Healthy Gaming Habits

Tips for maintaining a balanced approach to gaming

Establish clear time boundaries for gaming sessions. Use phone alarms or built-in device timers to remind yourself when to take breaks. The Pomodoro technique (25 minutes play, 5 minutes break) can be effective.

Daily Play Time 1 hour

Ensure gaming is part of a varied lifestyle that includes physical activity, social interaction, and other hobbies. Try the 50/30/20 rule:

  • 50% of leisure time for active/social activities
  • 30% for creative/learning pursuits
  • 20% for gaming/entertainment

Set and adhere to a budget for in-game purchases. Take advantage of parental controls if managing a child's gaming habits.
