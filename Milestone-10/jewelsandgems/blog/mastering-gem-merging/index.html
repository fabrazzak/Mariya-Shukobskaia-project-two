The 3-2-1 Merging Pattern

Most players merge gems as soon as they see a match, but top players use the 3-2-1 pattern to maintain board control:

Step 1: Build Three of a Kind

Instead of merging two gems immediately, wait until you have three of the same type. This creates a more valuable merged gem while leaving one original gem to continue the chain.

Step 2: Create Two Pairs

After your first merge, you'll have two gems left. Position these strategically to create two separate merging opportunities rather than one.

Step 3: Single Gem Placement

The remaining single gem should be placed where it can form the start of your next 3-2-1 sequence. This creates a continuous merging cycle.

Cascading Combos

Cascading occurs when one merge triggers another, which triggers another, creating a chain reaction. Here's how to set them up:

Vertical vs. Horizontal Cascades

Type How to Create Best Use
Vertical Cascades Merge gems in columns, allowing new gems to fall into existing matches When you need to clear the bottom of the board
Horizontal Cascades Create matches that push gems sideways into other matches For clearing specific rows or creating space
Diagonal Cascades Position gems so matches create diagonal reactions Advanced technique for maximum point combos

Strategic Gem Placement

Where you place your gems is just as important as what you merge:

Corner Strategy

The corners are powerful positions. Place higher-level gems here where they're less likely to be disturbed by random drops.

Center Control

The center of the board offers the most merging opportunities. Keep this area clear for maximum flexibility.

Edge Play

Use the edges to store gems you're building toward while working on other matches in the center.

Pro Tip

Try to always leave at least one empty space near your highest-level gem. This gives you room to maneuver when you're close to creating a special combination.

When to Hold vs. When to Merge

One of the most subtle but important skills is knowing when to merge immediately versus when to wait:

Merge Immediately When:

  • You need to clear space to prevent a board lock
  • The merge will trigger a valuable cascade
  • You're about to receive a new gem that would disrupt your setup

Hold Your Merge When:

  • You're one gem away from a higher-value combination
  • The current merge won't significantly improve your position
  • You're setting up a special gem combination

Advanced Booster Combinations

Boosters are powerful, but combined they're unstoppable:

Rainbow + Bomb

Using a rainbow gem to create multiple bombs can clear entire sections of the board.

Lightning + Multiplier

Chain lightning through multiplier gems for exponential point gains.

Time Freeze + Any Booster

Gives you unlimited moves to set up the perfect combination.
