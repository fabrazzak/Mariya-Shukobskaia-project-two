Recognizing Problem Gaming

While most people play games for fun, some may develop problematic behaviors. Here are some warning signs that gaming may be becoming a problem:

•

Preoccupation with Gaming

Thinking constantly about past gaming experiences or planning the next gaming session

•

Increasing Time Spent

Needing to play for longer periods to achieve the same level of excitement

•

Failed Attempts to Stop

Repeated unsuccessful efforts to control, cut back, or stop gaming

•

Restlessness or Irritability

Feeling restless or irritable when attempting to cut down or stop gaming

•

Gaming to Escape

Using gaming as a way to escape from problems or relieve negative moods

•

Lying About Gaming

Lying to family members or others to conceal the extent of gaming

•

Jeopardizing Relationships

Risking or losing significant relationships, jobs, or opportunities because of gaming

•

Relying on Others for Money

Relying on others to provide money to relieve desperate financial situations caused by gaming

Gaming Habits Self-Assessment

Answer these questions honestly to assess your gaming habits. The more "yes" answers, the more likely you may be developing problematic gaming behaviors.

1. Have you ever felt the need to bet more and more?

2. Have you ever lied to cover the amount of time or money you spent gaming?

3. Have you ever felt guilty about the way you game or what happens when you game?

4. Have you ever felt like you would like to stop, but didn't think you could?

5. Have you ever hidden betting slips, lottery tickets, or other gaming spending from others?
