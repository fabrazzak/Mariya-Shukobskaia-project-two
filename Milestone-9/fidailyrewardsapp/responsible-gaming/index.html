Responsible Gaming Play Smart, Play Safe

At FI Daily Rewards, we believe gaming should be fun and enjoyable. Learn how to maintain healthy gaming habits and recognize when it might be time to take a break.

Important Notice

While most people play games for fun without negative consequences, for some individuals gaming can become problematic. This page provides information to help you assess your gaming habits and find help if needed.

Warning Signs of Problematic Gaming

Be aware of these signs that may indicate gaming is becoming a problem:

Behavioral Signs

  • Spending increasing amounts of time gaming
  • Difficulty stopping or controlling gaming sessions
  • Neglecting work, school, or family responsibilities
  • Losing interest in other hobbies or activities
  • Continuing to play despite negative consequences

Emotional Signs

  • Feeling restless or irritable when not gaming
  • Using gaming to escape problems or relieve stress
  • Lying to family or friends about gaming time
  • Feeling guilty or defensive about gaming habits
  • Experiencing mood swings related to gaming

Self-Assessment Questions

Ask yourself these questions to evaluate your gaming habits:

  • Do I think about gaming even when I'm not playing?
  • Have I tried unsuccessfully to cut back on gaming?
  • Has gaming caused problems in my relationships?
  • Do I feel the need to play for increasing amounts of time?
  • Have I neglected personal hygiene or health due to gaming?

If you answered "yes" to several of these questions, you may want to evaluate your gaming habits more carefully.

Healthy Gaming Habits

Follow these tips to maintain a balanced approach to gaming:

Set Time Limits

Decide in advance how long you'll play and set a timer. Take regular breaks every 30-60 minutes.

Schedule Gaming

Make gaming part of a balanced schedule that includes other activities and responsibilities.

Avoid Gaming During Meals

Keep mealtimes screen-free to maintain healthy eating habits and social connections.

Prioritize Sleep

Stop gaming at least 1 hour before bedtime to ensure quality sleep.

Stay Active

Balance screen time with physical activity to maintain overall health.

Maintain Social Connections

Ensure gaming doesn't replace face-to-face interactions with friends and family.

Parental Guidance

For parents concerned about their child's gaming habits:

  • Set clear rules about when and how long children can play
  • Use parental controls to manage play time and content
  • Encourage a variety of activities beyond gaming
  • Monitor for signs of problematic gaming behavior
  • Play games together to understand their appeal
