Savory Cooking with Dark Chocolate

By Chef Alejandro Cortez April 12, 2025 7 min read

While chocolate is often relegated to the dessert menu, its complex flavor profile makes it a secret weapon in savory cooking. When used thoughtfully, premium dark chocolate can add depth, balance acidity, and create unforgettable umami richness in everything from sauces to rubs. Let's explore the culinary possibilities beyond the candy bar.

Essential Techniques

Shaving

Use a microplane to create chocolate curls for garnishing soups, salads, or pasta dishes.

Melting

Melt slowly into sauces or braising liquids, stirring constantly to prevent scorching.

Grinding

Combine with spices in a mortar or spice grinder for rubs and seasoning blends.

Signature Recipes

Oaxacan Black Mole

2 hrs 30 min Serves 6-8 Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 4 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 dried pasilla chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried chipotle chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 cups chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 corn tortilla, torn into pieces
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp cloves
  • 1/4 tsp cumin
  • 1/4 cup ChocoSol 85% Dark Chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Toast chiles in a dry skillet over medium heat for 1-2 minutes per side until fragrant but not burned. Transfer to a bowl and cover with hot water. Soak for 30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, toast sesame seeds, almonds, and raisins in the same skillet until golden. Set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, saut√© onion and garlic until softened. Add tortilla pieces and cook until slightly browned.
  4. Drain chiles, reserving 1 cup soaking liquid. Combine chiles, toasted ingredients, onion mixture, spices, and 1 cup stock in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  5. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the blended mixture and cook, stirring constantly, for 5 minutes.
  6. Add remaining stock and simmer for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add chopped chocolate and stir until melted. Continue simmering for another 30 minutes until thickened.
  8. Season with salt. Serve over chicken or turkey.

Chef's Notes

Authentic mole contains 20+ ingredients and takes days to prepare. This simplified version captures the essential flavors while being achievable for home cooks. The chocolate adds depth and helps balance the heat from the chiles.

Dark Chocolate Beef Chili

3 hrs Serves 6 Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ground beef (80/20)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15 oz) can kidney beans, drained
  • 1 (15 oz) can black beans, drained
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 2 oz ChocoSol 70% Dark Chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tbsp masa harina (optional, for thickening)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven, brown ground beef over medium-high heat. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute more.
  3. Stir in chili powder, cumin, paprika, and oregano. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, beans, and beef stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  5. Simmer uncovered for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add chopped chocolate and stir until melted. For thicker chili, stir in masa harina.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Serve with desired toppings.

Chef's Notes

The chocolate adds a subtle richness that elevates this classic chili. For extra depth, use half ground beef and half ground pork. The chili tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to meld.

Coffee-Chocolate Steak Rub

10 min Makes 1/2 cup Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp finely ground coffee
  • 2 tbsp ChocoSol Cocoa Powder
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

Instructions

  1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix thoroughly.
  2. Store in an airtight container for up to 3 months.
  3. To use: Generously coat steak (preferably ribeye or strip) with rub and let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before grilling.
  4. Grill to desired doneness, allowing a nice crust to form from the sugar in the rub.
  5. Let rest for 5 minutes before slicing.

Chef's Notes

This rub creates an incredible crust with layers of flavor - the coffee and chocolate enhance the meat's natural savoriness while the sugar promotes caramelization. Works equally well on pork chops or portobello mushrooms for a vegetarian option.
