Top 5 Team Compositions for Late Game Progression

As you progress into the late game stages of Everybody's RPG: Reboot, team composition becomes increasingly important. The right combination of heroes can mean the difference between being stuck at stage 500 or pushing through to 600 and beyond. After extensive testing and community feedback, we've identified the top 5 most effective team compositions for late game progression in the current meta.

1. The Eternal Guardians (Defensive Sustain)

The Eternal Guardians

Medium Difficulty

This composition focuses on outlasting enemies through powerful defensive abilities and healing. Ideal for players who prefer a slower, more methodical approach to battles.

Main Tank
Terra Guardian
Primary Healer
Celestial Priestess
Support
Ancient Scholar
DPS
Frost Archer
DPS
Ember Mage

Synergy Breakdown

Terra Guardian provides massive damage reduction with his stone barrier ability, while Celestial Priestess keeps everyone healthy with her area heals. Ancient Scholar boosts the team's energy regeneration, allowing for more frequent ultimate abilities. The two DPS heroes provide consistent damage from range while being protected.

Pros

  • Extremely durable against all enemy types
  • Consistent performance in all content
  • Forgiving for timing mistakes
  • Easy to gear with defensive stats

Cons

  • Slower clear times than burst teams
  • Requires specific legendary heroes
  • Struggles against timed challenges

Pro Tip

Prioritize cooldown reduction on Celestial Priestess and Ancient Scholar to maximize uptime on their crucial support abilities. Terra Guardian should focus on pure defense stats to make the most of his barrier scaling.

2. The Shadow Assassins (Burst Damage)

The Shadow Assassins

Hard Difficulty

This high-risk, high-reward composition focuses on eliminating enemies before they can become a threat. Requires precise timing but offers the fastest clear times when executed properly.

Off-Tank
Phantom Blade
Main DPS
Night Stalker
Burst DPS
Void Assassin
Support
Shadow Weaver
Secondary Healer
Blood Priest

Synergy Breakdown

Phantom Blade provides just enough survivability while dealing respectable damage. Night Stalker and Void Assassin combo their ultimate abilities to delete priority targets. Shadow Weaver amplifies the team's damage with vulnerability debuffs, while Blood Priest offers emergency healing through lifesteal effects rather than direct heals.

Pros

  • Fastest clear times when optimized
  • Excels in timed challenges
  • Can punch above weight class in damage
  • Very fun and active playstyle

Cons

  • Requires precise ability timing
  • Vulnerable to crowd control
  • Struggles against high-health enemies
  • Demanding gear requirements

Pro Tip

Time Void Assassin's ultimate to land just after Night Stalker applies his armor shred debuff. This combo can often one-shot even the tankiest enemies. Focus on critical hit chance and damage for all DPS heroes in this composition.

3. The Elemental Masters (Crowd Control)

The Elemental Masters

Medium Difficulty

This team locks down enemies with constant crowd control while dealing sustained elemental damage. Excellent for dealing with large groups of enemies or particularly dangerous foes.

Main Tank
Storm Warden
DPS/CC
Frost Witch
DPS/CC
Ember Mage
Support
Tide Caller
Healer
Nature's Prophet

Synergy Breakdown

Storm Warden provides frontline stability with his knockbacks and stuns. Frost Witch and Ember Mage alternate freeze and burn effects to keep enemies controlled while dealing damage. Tide Caller amplifies all elemental damage and provides additional crowd control, while Nature's Prophet keeps everyone healthy with her healing over time abilities.

Pros

  • Excellent crowd control capabilities
  • Strong against groups of enemies
  • Flexible positioning options
  • Good mix of offense and defense

Cons

  • CC-resistant enemies can be problematic
  • Requires careful ability sequencing
  • Medium gear requirements

Pro Tip

Alternate Frost Witch's freeze with Ember Mage's burn effects to trigger elemental reactions for bonus damage. Tide Caller's ultimate should be saved for when multiple enemies are gathered together to maximize its impact.

4. The Summoner's Circle (Minion Army)

The Summoner's Circle

Easy Difficulty

This unique composition overwhelms enemies with numbers, summoning powerful minions to fight alongside your heroes. Great for beginners and those who enjoy a more passive playstyle.

Tank/Summoner
Beast Master
Summoner DPS

Summoner DPS

Synergy Breakdown

Beast Master summons powerful animal companions to tank and deal damage. Necromancer raises undead minions from fallen enemies, while Artificer deploys mechanical turrets. Bard buffs all summoned units with attack speed and damage increases, and Druid provides area heals that affect both heroes and summons.

Pros

  • Very beginner friendly
  • Minions absorb enemy attacks
  • Scales well with investment
  • Good for AFK farming

Cons

  • Vulnerable to area damage
  • Can be RNG dependent
  • Slower single-target damage

Pro Tip

Focus on ability duration and cooldown reduction for your summoners to maintain maximum uptime on their minions. The Bard's buffs are crucial - prioritize leveling his skills after your main summoners.

5. The Critical Mass (Crit Focus)

The Critical Mass

Hard Difficulty

This high-risk, high-reward composition focuses entirely on critical hits, dealing massive damage when everything aligns properly. Not for the faint of heart but incredibly satisfying when it works.

Off-Tank
Blade Dancer
Main DPS

Synergy Breakdown

Blade Dancer provides frontline presence while also benefiting from critical hits. Sharpshooter and Assassin deal massive critical damage, especially when their abilities are timed together. Fortune Teller boosts the entire team's critical chance and damage, while Battle Medic provides burst healing that can crit for extra value.

Pros

  • Highest potential damage output
  • Extremely satisfying crit chains
  • Excels in boss fights
  • Good synergy between heroes

Cons

  • Very gear dependent
  • RNG can screw you over
  • No crowd control
  • Difficult to play optimally

Pro Tip

Stack critical hit chance to at least 60% on your DPS heroes before focusing on critical damage. Fortune Teller's ultimate should be saved for when both DPS heroes are ready to unload their abilities for maximum impact.

Choosing the Right Composition

While all of these team compositions are viable for late game content, the best one for you depends on several factors:

  • Available Heroes: Work with what you have rather than forcing a composition you're missing key pieces for
  • Playstyle Preference: Do you prefer active gameplay or more passive approaches?
  • Content Focus: Some teams excel in different types of content (campaign, dungeons, PvP, etc.)
  • Gear Availability: Certain compositions require specific stat priorities on gear

Remember that the meta is always shifting with balance changes and new hero releases. These compositions represent the current state of the game but may need adjustment in future updates. The most important thing is to find a team that you enjoy playing and that fits your available resources.
