Behind the Scenes: Creating the Rally Experience

Sarah Williams 14 Comments March 22, 2025
Home » Blog » Developer Insights

Creating an authentic rally experience while maintaining accessible gameplay was our biggest challenge when developing Art of Rally. As the lead developer, I want to share some insights into how we approached the physics, track design, and visual style that make the game unique.

The Physics Model

Our goal was to capture the essence of rally physics without overwhelming players with excessive realism. Here's how we balanced authenticity with fun:

Weight Transfer

We implemented a simplified but effective weight transfer system that makes Scandinavian flicks and pendulum turns possible without requiring a physics PhD to execute.

Tire Model

Rather than simulating individual tire compounds, we created three surface types (tarmac, gravel, snow) with distinct grip characteristics that change dynamically with weather.

Drivetrain Differences

Each drivetrain type (FWD, RWD, 4WD) has distinct handling characteristics that reflect real-world behavior while remaining fun to drive.

Track Design Philosophy

Our stages are inspired by real rally locations but designed for optimal gameplay flow:

Key Principles

  • Flow: Every corner leads naturally to the next
  • Variety: Mix of technical and high-speed sections
  • Landmarks: Distinct visual cues for pace notes
  • Risk/Reward: Dangerous cuts that can save time

Iteration Process

Each track went through 10-15 iterations where we:

  1. Blocked out the basic layout
  2. Tested driving flow
  3. Adjusted camber and sightlines
  4. Added environmental details
  5. Balanced difficulty

Lessons Learned

Looking back on development, several key lessons emerged:

Pre-Production

Scope Carefully

We initially planned too many car models and had to scale back to maintain quality.

Early Alpha

Test Early, Test Often

Getting community feedback during development helped us identify which realism compromises worked.

Beta

Accessibility Matters

Adding multiple control schemes (tilt, touch, controller) significantly expanded our audience.

What's Next?

We're currently working on new features based on player feedback:

  • Dynamic Weather: Rain that affects grip progressively as stages continue
  • Improved Replays: More camera options and sharing features
  • Community Events: Weekly challenges with special conditions
