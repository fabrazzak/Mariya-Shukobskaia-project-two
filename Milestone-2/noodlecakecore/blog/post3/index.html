Mastering car setup in Art of Rally can mean the difference between barely making a corner and perfectly executing a Scandinavian flick. This advanced guide will walk you through suspension tuning, gear ratios, differential settings, and more to help you optimize your car for any stage.

Understanding Setup Basics

Before diving into specific settings, it's important to understand how changes affect your car's behavior:

Trade-offs

Every adjustment has pros and cons. Stiffer suspension improves cornering but reduces bump absorption.

Stage Conditions

Consider surface type (tarmac, gravel, snow), weather, and stage layout when configuring your car.

Car Characteristics

Front-engine, rear-engine, and 4WD cars require different approaches to setup.

Suspension Tuning

The suspension system controls how your car interacts with the road surface. Here's how to optimize it:

Spring Rate

Controls how stiff your suspension is. Higher values mean less body roll but a harsher ride.

  • Tarmac: Higher spring rates (70-90%)
  • Gravel: Medium rates (50-70%)
  • Snow: Lower rates (40-60%)

Damping

Controls how quickly the suspension reacts to bumps. Two settings:

  • Rebound: How fast the suspension extends after compression
  • Bump: How fast the suspension compresses

For loose surfaces, use slower damping to maintain traction. On smooth tarmac, faster damping improves responsiveness.

Anti-Roll Bars

Controls how much the car resists rolling in corners. Affects balance between understeer and oversteer.

  • Front-heavy: Softer front bar reduces understeer
  • Rear-heavy: Softer rear bar reduces oversteer
  • 4WD: Balanced settings work best

Gear Ratios

Proper gearing ensures your engine stays in its optimal power band throughout the stage.

Gear Ratio Strategies

  • Short ratios: Better acceleration, good for twisty stages
  • Long ratios: Higher top speed, good for fast stages
  • Custom: Adjust individual gears for specific needs

Use the final drive ratio to adjust overall gearing without changing individual gears.

Differential Settings

The differential controls how power is distributed between wheels. Art of Rally features three adjustable differentials:

Front Differential

Open Locked

Controls power distribution between front wheels. More lock improves traction but can cause understeer.

Rear Differential

Open Locked

Controls power distribution between rear wheels. More lock improves traction but can cause oversteer.

Center Differential (4WD)

RWD Bias FWD Bias

Controls front/rear power split. More rear bias improves rotation, more front bias improves stability.

Setup Presets

Here are some proven setup configurations for different conditions:

  • Spring Rate: Front 80%, Rear 75%
  • Damping: Fast rebound, medium bump
  • Anti-Roll Bars: Front 70%, Rear 65%
  • Gearing: Short 1st-3rd, long 4th-5th
  • Differential: Front 60%, Rear 70%, Center 50%
  • Spring Rate: Front 60%, Rear 55%
  • Damping: Medium rebound, slow bump
  • Anti-Roll Bars: Front 50%, Rear 45%
  • Gearing: Even spacing, slightly short
  • Differential: Front 50%, Rear 60%, Center 45%
  • Spring Rate: Front 45%, Rear 40%
  • Damping: Slow rebound, slow bump
  • Anti-Roll Bars: Front 40%, Rear 35%
  • Gearing: Long ratios to prevent wheelspin
  • Differential: Front 40%, Rear 50%, Center 40%
