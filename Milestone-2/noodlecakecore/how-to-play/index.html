How to Play Art of Rally

Master the controls, techniques, and strategies to become a rally champion

Home » How to Play

Basic Controls

Steering

Tilt your device or use the on-screen steering wheel to control your car's direction. Smooth inputs work best for maintaining control.

Acceleration

Press and hold the right side of the screen to accelerate. The longer you press, the faster you'll go.

Braking

Press the left side of the screen to brake. Tap quickly for light braking or hold for full stops.

Handbrake

Swipe down with two fingers to engage the handbrake for sharp turns and drifts.

Driving Techniques

This advanced technique helps you take sharp corners more effectively:

  1. Approach the turn slightly from the opposite side of the road
  2. Just before the turn, steer briefly away from the corner
  3. Quickly turn into the corner while applying the handbrake
  4. Control the slide with counter-steering
  5. Accelerate out of the turn

Understanding weight transfer is crucial for maintaining control:

  • Braking: Shifts weight to the front wheels, improving turn-in
  • Accelerating: Shifts weight to the rear, increasing traction
  • Lift-off oversteer: Releasing throttle mid-corner can help rotate the car
  • Trail braking: Light braking while turning can improve corner entry

Learn to interpret the co-driver's pace notes:

Call Meaning Action
"1" Very sharp turn Brake hard, slow down significantly
"2" Medium turn Lift off throttle, prepare to turn
"3" Gentle curve Slight steering adjustment
"Left/Right" Turn direction Steer accordingly
"Into" Tightens Prepare for decreasing radius

Pro Tips

01

Look Ahead

Don't focus just in front of your car. Look further down the road to anticipate turns and obstacles.

02

Brake Early

It's better to brake too early than too late. Finish braking before you start turning.

03

Smooth Inputs

Rally is about finesse. Smooth steering and throttle control are faster than jerky movements.

04

Learn the Stages

Repetition is key. The more you drive a stage, the better you'll remember its challenges.

05

Car Setup

Experiment with different car setups. Softer suspension works better on rough terrain.

06

Weather Matters

Adjust your driving for different conditions. Wet surfaces require gentler inputs.
