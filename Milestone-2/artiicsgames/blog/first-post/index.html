La Liga 2024-25 Season: Team-by-Team Preview

David Martinez May 15, 2024 • 8 min read
La Liga trophy with football on pitch (Photo: La Liga)

The 2024-25 La Liga season promises to be one of the most exciting in recent years, with several teams strengthening their squads and new managers bringing fresh ideas. In this comprehensive preview, we analyze all 20 teams, their key signings, tactical approaches, and what fans can expect in the upcoming campaign.

The Title Contenders

Real Madrid

After securing their 36th La Liga title last season, Real Madrid enter as favorites once again. The addition of French superstar Kylian Mbappé gives them arguably the most fearsome attack in Europe, joining Vinícius Júnior and Rodrygo in a devastating front three. Carlo Ancelotti's 4-3-3 system has been finely tuned, with Jude Bellingham continuing his development as one of the world's best midfielders.

FC Barcelona

Xavi's second full season in charge sees Barcelona looking to close the gap to their eternal rivals. The emergence of young talents like Lamine Yamal and Pau Cubarsí has been a bright spot, while the experienced Robert Lewandowski continues to deliver goals. Their financial situation remains challenging, but smart free transfers like Manchester City's İlkay Gündoğan last summer show they can still attract quality.

Atlético Madrid

Diego Simeone's side can never be counted out, with their trademark defensive solidity and counter-attacking prowess. Antoine Griezmann remains the creative heartbeat, while Álvaro Morata enjoyed a career-best goalscoring season last year. The potential departure of Jan Oblak could be a significant blow unless they find an adequate replacement.

European Hopefuls

Real Sociedad

Imanol Alguacil's attractive brand of football earned them Champions League qualification last season. The Basque club has done well to retain most of their key players, with Takefusa Kubo developing into one of La Liga's most exciting wingers.

Villarreal

After a disappointing mid-table finish last season, Villarreal have appointed Marcelino as manager, returning to the club where he enjoyed great success. Their squad boasts quality throughout, particularly in attack with Alexander Sørloth and Gerard Moreno.

Tactical Insight: Villarreal Under Marcelino

Marcelino typically employs a 4-4-2 system with an emphasis on defensive organization and quick transitions. Expect Villarreal to be much harder to break down this season while maintaining their attacking threat through direct wing play.

Relegation Battle

The newly promoted teams - Leganés, Espanyol, and Racing Santander - will all be looking to avoid an immediate return to the second division. Of the three, Espanyol have the most top-flight experience and strongest squad on paper.

Among last season's survivors, Cádiz and Mallorca will likely be in the relegation mix again unless they can improve their goalscoring output. Cádiz in particular scored just 25 goals in 38 games last term - the lowest in the division.

Players to Watch

  • Kylian Mbappé (Real Madrid): The world's most exciting player finally makes his La Liga debut
  • Lamine Yamal (Barcelona): At just 17, the winger could become a regular starter
  • Arda Güler (Real Madrid): After an injury-hit first season, the Turkish wonderkid is ready to shine
  • Samu Omorodion (Atlético Madrid): The young striker impressed on loan at Alavés last season

Season Predictions

The 2024-25 La Liga season promises drama at both ends of the table. While Real Madrid are clear favorites, Barcelona and Atlético will push them hard. The battle for European places looks particularly competitive, with several clubs capable of challenging. One thing is certain - with world-class talent throughout the league, Spanish football fans are in for another thrilling campaign.
